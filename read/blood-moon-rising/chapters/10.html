Chapter 10: The Price of Trust

~1.8k words · Updated 2026-06-22

Luna's words hung in the air like smoke. *Your father's mate.* Liam's bloodied fingers curled into the stream bed, pebbles digging into his palms. The water rushed past him, cold and indifferent, carrying away the evidence of his transformation.

"Marcus never mentioned you."

"Marcus didn't know everything." She kept her hand extended. Steady. Patient. "Alexander kept me hidden. For good reason."

Liam pushed himself up. His body screamed in protest. Every muscle felt like it had been torn apart and hastily reassembled. The mark on his chest pulsed with a dull ache, the crescent moon and wolf's head still glowing faintly through his torn shirt.

"Hidden from the Council?"

"Hidden from everyone." Her eyes flickered with something ancient. Pain, maybe. Or regret. "If the Council knew Alexander had a mate—a true mate—they would have used me to control him. So we kept our bond secret. Met in shadows. Loved in silence."

Liam stood. His legs wobbled. The stream rushed around his ankles, numbing his wounds. "And now? You show up the night his son nearly dies?"

"Because you're out of time."

A growl built in his chest. Not his. Alexander's. The Wolf King's blood recognized its mate's scent, even if Liam didn't understand it. *She's telling the truth,* the voice rumbled. *I can smell it.*

*Shut up,* Liam thought back. *You're not in charge here.*

*Yet.*

He ignored the voice and focused on Luna. Her silver hair caught the moonlight like spun glass. Her eyes held galaxies. She was beautiful in a way that transcended human standards—beautiful in a way that made his wolf want to submit.

He hated that.

"The Moonlit Temple," he said. "What is it?"

"A sanctuary. A training ground. The only place where the Wolf King's heir can learn to control the bloodline without the Council finding you." She lowered her hand. "Alexander prepared it before he died. For you."

"Prepared it how?"

"Ancient wards. Deadman's triggers. A failsafe that activates when the bloodline passes to the next heir." She paused. "It's been waiting thirty years for you to wake up."

Liam looked around. The forest pressed in from all sides, dark and alive. Somewhere in the distance, he heard howling. Not wolves. Werewolves. The rogue pack from the warehouse, probably. Or the Council's enforcers. Or both.

"How far?"

"Three hours. If we run."

"I can barely walk."

"Then I'll carry you." She said it without hesitation. Without irony. Like it was the most natural thing in the world.

"Not happening."

"Liam—"

"I said no." He stepped past her, nearly fell, caught himself on a tree trunk. The bark scraped his palm raw. "I don't know you. I don't trust you. For all I know, you're the one who sent those rogues to flush me out."

"Then why did I save you in the alley?"

"To earn my trust."

She laughed. It was a sad sound, hollow and ancient. "You sound like him. Alexander. Always questioning. Always suspicious." She walked closer. Close enough that he could smell her scent—jasmine and moonlit rain and something else. Something that made his chest ache. "I loved your father. I watched him die. I've spent thirty years waiting for you to wake up so I could finish what he started."

"What did he start?"

"The war."

The word hit him like a punch. "What war?"

Luna's expression hardened. "The Council isn't just a group of elders who keep order. They're a regime. They've controlled every werewolf bloodline for centuries, culling anyone who threatens their power. Alexander was the first Wolf King in a hundred years who refused to bow. He built an army. He trained warriors. He was days away from striking when they killed him."

"Days away?"

"They poisoned him. Silver-laced wine at a peace summit. It took him three hours to die." Her voice cracked. Just slightly. Just enough. "He spent those three hours writing instructions. Mapping out the Temple. Making sure you would have a chance."

Liam's hand drifted to his chest. The mark was warm now. Almost hot. *She's not lying,* Alexander said. *I remember the wine. I remember the taste.*

*You remember dying?*

*I remember everything.*

Liam closed his eyes. The forest swayed around him. The howling grew closer. "If I go with you, what happens?"

"You train. You learn to control the shift. You master the bloodline."

"And then?"

"Then you decide." She met his gaze. "You can hide. Live in the shadows, always running. Or you can finish what Alexander started. Take the war to the Council. Burn their regime to ash."

"And if I choose to hide?"

"Then I've wasted thirty years."

The howling was closer now. Definitely closer. Liam could smell them—five, maybe six wolves, moving fast, tracking something. Tracking him.

"Fine," he said. "Take me to the Temple."

Luna nodded. She turned, her silver hair catching the wind, and started walking. "Try to keep up."

She moved through the forest like a ghost. Silent. Fluid. Her feet barely touched the ground. Liam followed, stumbling, crashing through branches, leaving a trail a blind human could follow.

"You're too loud," she said without turning.

"I'm dying."

"You're whining."

"I'm doing both."

She stopped. Turned. Studied him with those ancient eyes. "The bloodline is fighting you. Stop resisting and it will guide your body."

"I don't know how to stop resisting."

"Then learn." She stepped closer. Her hand touched his chest, right over the mark. Her fingers were cold. "Close your eyes."

He hesitated.

"Do it."

He closed his eyes. Her hand pressed harder. Heat spread through his chest, radiating outward, into his arms, his legs, his spine. The pain didn't disappear, but it changed. Became something else. Something he could work with.

"Your father used to say the wolf isn't a monster," she whispered. "It's a mirror. It shows you what you really are. The fear. The rage. The love. Everything you try to hide. It forces you to look."

"I don't want to look."

"I know." Her hand dropped. "But the Blood Moon doesn't care what you want."

She turned and started walking again. Faster now. Liam followed. His legs felt stronger. His lungs burned less. The mark on his chest pulsed like a second heartbeat.

They ran for what felt like hours. The forest blurred past. Trees became shadows. Shadows became trees. The howling faded, then grew louder, then faded again. Liam lost track of time. Lost track of direction. Lost track of everything except the silver hair ahead of him, guiding him through the darkness.

Then the forest opened.

A clearing. Stone ruins. A crumbling archway covered in vines and moss. Moonlight poured through the center, pooling on the ground like liquid silver.

"The Temple," Luna said.

"This is it?"

"This is the entrance." She walked toward the archway. "The real Temple is underground. Your father built it beneath the ruins. Wards hide it from every supernatural radar. No one can find it unless they know where to look."

"How did you know where to look?"

"I helped build it."

She touched the archway. The vines recoiled. The moss peeled away. Symbols carved into the stone began to glow—crescent moons and wolf's heads and patterns Liam didn't recognize.

The ground trembled.

A crack split the center of the clearing. The grass parted. Stone shifted. A staircase spiraled downward, disappearing into darkness. Light flickered from below. Ancient. Warm. Welcoming.

"The Temple recognizes the bloodline," Luna said. "It knows you're here."

Liam stared into the darkness. His father had built this. His father had died for this. Thirty years, waiting for a son he never met to wake up and claim his legacy.

"What if I'm not ready?" Liam asked.

"You're not." Luna stepped onto the first stair. "But you have six days to become ready."

She descended. Liam followed. The staircase wound deeper and deeper, spiraling into the earth. The walls were lined with carvings—battles, kings, wolves with eyes like burning stars. History. His history. The history of every Wolf King who had come before him.

At the bottom, a door. Iron. Massive. Covered in the same symbols as the archway above.

Luna pressed her palm to the center. The symbols flared. The door groaned. It swung open, revealing a chamber that took Liam's breath away.

A library. Thousands of books, stacked floor to ceiling. Weapons on the walls—silver swords, obsidian daggers, bows carved from bone. Training dummies in the corner, scarred and battered. A fireplace crackled at the far end, casting shadows that danced like living things.

And in the center of the room, a pedestal. On top of it, a sword. Black blade. Silver hilt. A crescent moon etched into the crossguard.

"Your father's blade," Luna said. "Moonfang. It's been waiting for you."

Liam walked toward it. His hand reached out. His fingers brushed the hilt.

The world exploded.

Pain. Vision. Sound. Everything at once. He saw his father's face—not in a photograph, not in a memory, but *real*. Alive. Alexander stood before him, tall and strong, eyes burning with the same fire Liam felt in his chest.

*Son.*

The word echoed through his skull.

*You have six days. The Council knows you're alive. The Blood Moon is coming. And somewhere in this Temple, I left you everything you need to win.*

Alexander smiled. It was sad. Ancient. Familiar.

*But first, you have to survive the night.*

The vision shattered.

Liam stumbled backward, gasping. The sword clattered to the floor. The mark on his chest blazed like a brand.

"What happened?" Luna grabbed his shoulders. "What did you see?"

"My father." Liam's voice shook. "He said I have to survive the night."

Luna's face went pale. "The wards—" She spun around. Her eyes scanned the walls. "Something triggered them."

A crash echoed from above. The archway. Someone was breaking through.

"How did they find us?" Liam demanded.

Luna's expression twisted. Guilt. Fear. Something else he couldn't name. "Because the Temple has another failsafe. One Alexander built without telling me."

"What failsafe?"

She met his eyes. "When the heir touches Moonfang, it sends a signal. A beacon. Every ally. Every enemy. Everyone with Wolf King blood will know where you are."

"You're saying my father made me a target?"

"I'm saying your father trusted that you would be strong enough to survive."

Another crash. Louder. Closer. The door at the top of the stairs splintered.

Liam grabbed Moonfang. The blade hummed in his hand. Alive. Hungry.

"Six days," he whispered.

Footsteps thundered down the staircase.

Luna positioned herself beside him, her body shifting, her eyes glowing silver. "Ready?"

"Not even close."

The door exploded inward.

And the night began.
