📖 Blood Moon Rising: The Wolf King's Awakening by Fenrir Shadow

Chapter 4: The Taste of Moon-Steel

~1.7k words · Updated 2026-06-22

The shower ran cold for ten minutes before Liam dared step out.

His reflection stared back from the fogged mirror. Same brown eyes. Same sharp jaw. Same scar above his left eyebrow from the time he'd fallen out of a tree at twelve. But something had shifted behind those eyes. Something ancient and patient that watched the world with ancient hunger.

He pressed a hand to his chest. The skin was smooth now. No wound. No scar. Just the faint outline of a tattoo he'd never gotten — a crescent moon wrapped in chains, dark as spilled ink.

*That's new.*

The bathroom door rattled. "Liam? You okay in there? You've been in for like an hour."

Marcus. His roommate since freshman year. The guy who ate instant noodles at 3 AM and left his socks everywhere.

"Yeah. Coming out."

Liam wrapped a towel around his waist and opened the door. Marcus stood there holding a bag of chips, mouth half-open.

"Dude. You look... different."

"Didn't sleep well."

"No, I mean —" Marcus waved the chips at him. "You look like you hit the gym for three years in one night. Your shoulders. Your jaw. Even your eyes are doing that thing."

"What thing?"

"The thing where you look like you could kill someone and smile about it."

Liam walked past him to his bedroom. "I'm fine."

"You were screaming last night. Like, *screaming* screaming. I almost called 911."

Liam stopped. His hands gripped the doorframe. "I don't remember."

"You never do. That's the problem." Marcus's voice went quiet. "It's been happening since you turned twenty-one. Every full moon. You lock yourself in your room and I hear —" He swallowed. "I hear things I can't explain. Cracking bones. Growling. Last night I saw shadows moving under your door that didn't make sense."

*He knows more than he's saying.*

Liam turned around. "Marcus. Have you seen this before? Before college?"

Marcus's face went pale. He looked at the floor. "I was told not to say anything."

"Told by who?"

"Your grandmother."

The words hit like a punch. His grandmother had died when he was sixteen. Cancer. He'd held her hand while she went. But in her final weeks, she'd gripped his wrist with surprising strength and whispered: *When the moon calls, don't answer. Not until you're ready.*

He'd thought it was morphine talking.

"When did she tell you this?"

"Freshman orientation. She showed up at my dorm. Said you'd need a friend who understood, even if you didn't." Marcus laughed bitterly. "I thought she was crazy. A sweet old lady with dementia. But then the first full moon hit and I heard you howling."

Liam's blood ran cold. "Howling."

"Like a wolf. A big one." Marcus's hands were shaking. "I didn't sleep for a week. But she'd made me promise. She said if I told you too early, it would *destroy you.* That you had to come into it naturally or the bloodline would reject you and you'd die."

*Die.*

The word echoed in Liam's skull. He remembered the darkness from last night. The hunger. The power that had torn through his veins like wildfire. And somewhere in that chaos, he'd felt something fighting him. Something that wanted to take over permanently.

"Is there more?"

Marcus nodded slowly. "She gave me a box. Said to give it to you when you started asking questions."

"Where is it?"

"Under my bed. I've been hiding it for three years."

---

The box was made of dark wood, bound in silver straps engraved with symbols that seemed to move when Liam looked at them directly. No lock. Just a clasp shaped like a wolf's head with ruby eyes.

His fingers touched the silver.

Pain exploded through his hand. He jerked back, hissing. The skin on his fingertips was red, blistered.

"Silver burns?" Marcus whispered.

"Apparently."

Liam looked at his burned fingers. They were already healing, the blisters shrinking, new skin forming. His grandmother had known. She'd planned for this.

"Get me a towel. I'll open it without touching the silver."

Marcus handed him a t-shirt. Liam wrapped it around his hand, flipped the wolf-head clasp, and lifted the lid.

Inside lay three things.

A leather journal, worn and stained. A silver dagger with a blade that seemed to absorb light. And a folded letter addressed in his grandmother's handwriting:

*For Liam. When the Wolf King awakens.*

He opened the letter with trembling hands.

*My dearest grandson,*

*If you're reading this, you've survived the first Awakening. I wish I could have been there to guide you, but the old blood has rules that even I cannot break. You had to face the wolf alone, prove you were strong enough to hold the leash.*

*You did. I knew you would.*

*By now you've noticed changes. The strength. The healing. The hunger that whispers at the back of your mind. That is the Wolf King bloodline — the oldest and most powerful of the werewolf clans. We have guarded this secret for three hundred years, waiting for the right vessel.*

*That vessel is you.*

*But power attracts predators. There are other packs who would kill to claim your blood. The Silver Fang clan has hunted our family for generations. They know the Awakening is coming. They will find you.*

*You have seven days until the Blood Moon. On that night, you must complete the Ritual of Ascension at the Moonstone Altar in our ancestral village. Fail, and your soul belongs to the wolf forever. You will become a mindless beast, driven by instinct and hunger.*

*I have left you tools. The journal contains everything I know. The dagger is made of moon-steel — the only weapon that can kill a werewolf. Carry it always.*

*The last thing: trust no one. The Silver Fang has spies everywhere. Even your closest friends may be enemies.*

*But there is one who will help you. A woman marked by the moon. She will find you when the time is right.*

*I love you, Liam. I always have. And I am sorry I could not give you a normal life.*

*Forgive me.*

*— Grandmother*

Liam read the letter three times. His hands didn't stop shaking until the third reading.

"A woman marked by the moon?" Marcus leaned over his shoulder. "Sounds romantic."

"This isn't a joke."

"I know. I'm scared too." Marcus pointed at the dagger. "But you're going to need help. And I'm your roommate. That makes me your pack now."

Something warm kindled in Liam's chest. He looked at Marcus — the guy who left his socks everywhere, who ate noodles at 3 AM, who had kept a secret for three years because an old woman asked him to.

"Thank you."

"Don't thank me yet. We have a lot of research to do." Marcus picked up the journal. "And I'm not letting you face this alone."

The door exploded inward.

Liam moved without thinking. He grabbed Marcus, shoved him behind the bed, and drew the moon-steel dagger in one fluid motion. The blade sang as it left the sheath.

Three men stood in the doorway. They wore black suits that did nothing to hide the bulk of their bodies. Their eyes glowed yellow in the dim light. Their teeth — when they smiled — were too sharp.

"Liam Chen," the lead man said. His voice was gravel and broken glass. "The Wolf King awakens, and he's a college student with a roommate." He laughed. "How disappointing."

"Who are you?"

"Silver Fang. We've been watching you since birth." He stepped forward. "Your grandmother killed my father. It's only fair I return the favor with her grandson."

Liam felt the wolf stir inside him. The hunger. The rage. The whisper of ancient power begging to be unleashed.

*Give me control. I'll kill them all.*

*No.*

*They'll kill you. Let me out.*

*I said no.*

The lead man pulled a blade from his coat — silver, like his grandmother's. "This will hurt. I promise."

Marcus threw a lamp at his face.

The man dodged, but it bought them two seconds. Liam grabbed Marcus's arm and ran for the window. Three stories down. Concrete below.

"Are you crazy?!"

"We're werewolves now!" Liam kicked the window frame, shattering glass and wood. "Jump with me!"

Marcus stared at the drop. "I'm NOT a werewolf!"

The men were closing in. Liam grabbed Marcus, pulled him close, and leaped.

They hit the ground hard. Liam felt his ankle snap, then heal, snap again, heal again. Marcus groaned beneath him, alive but bleeding from a cut on his forehead.

"Run."

They ran.

Through alleys, over fences, across streets. Liam's legs moved faster than they ever had. He smelled everything — the garbage in the dumpsters, the exhaust from cars, the sweat on Marcus's skin. He heard the Silver Fang chasing them, their footsteps rhythmic and predatory.

They burst into a park. Empty. Dark. The moon hung overhead, almost full.

*Three more days until the Blood Moon.*

A figure stood at the center of the park. A woman. Long black hair. Silver eyes that glowed in the moonlight. She wore a leather jacket and carried a blade that matched his.

The woman marked by the moon.

"You're late, Wolf King," she said. "And you brought friends."

Liam's wolf howled in recognition. *Mate.*

The Silver Fang emerged from the treeline behind them. Six now. More than he could fight.

The woman smiled. "I'll handle this. Go to the address in the journal. I'll find you when it's safe."

"How do I know I can trust you?"

She met his eyes. Something ancient passed between them. A bond that had existed before either of them was born.

"Because I've been waiting for you my whole life, Liam. And I'm not about to let them take you now."

The Silver Fang charged.

She moved like water, like wind, like moonlight made flesh. Her blade caught the first man's throat before he could scream.

"GO!"

Liam grabbed Marcus and ran.

Behind them, the sounds of battle echoed through the night. Snarls. Cries. The wet sound of steel meeting flesh.

And as they disappeared into the darkness, Liam heard the woman's voice in his head, clear as a bell:

*Find the Moonstone Altar. I'll keep them busy. But Liam?*

*You have less time than you think.*

*The Blood Moon rises in three days.*

*And the wolf is already hungry.*
