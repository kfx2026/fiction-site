Chapter 1: The Crippled Snake's Secret

~1.9k words · 2026-06-22

The Ceremonial Grounds of Thousand Beast Valley stretched across a plateau of white jade, where three thousand young disciples stood in perfect formation beneath a sky the color of bruised plums. Lin Xiao's palms were slick with sweat as he watched the elders file onto the central dais, their robes embroidered with beasts that seemed to move in the corner of your eye—serpents coiling, wolves stalking, eagles diving.

He'd waited sixteen years for this moment.

"Today, you become beast masters," Elder Shen announced, his voice carrying across the plateau without effort, the mark of a cultivator at the fourth stage of the Beast Heart Realm. "The bond you form this morning will shape your entire path of cultivation. Choose wisely. Cherish deeply. The beast that answers your call will be your partner for life."

Lin Xiao's heart hammered against his ribs. He'd memorized every beast manual in the sect's library, studied the strengths and weaknesses of every known spirit beast in the valley. He knew the lightning hawk could sense qi fluctuations from three miles away. He knew the shadow panther could merge with darkness itself. He knew the iron-backed bear could shatter boulders with a single swipe.

He wanted any of them. All of them. But he'd settle for one.

"First formation, step forward!"

The disciples ahead of him moved with the confidence of those who'd already won. Lin Xiao watched as a girl from the Azure Peak branch summoned a crystal fox, its fur shimmering like morning frost. A boy from the same branch called forth a bronze-scaled serpent that wrapped around his arm like living armor. Each successful bond drew murmurs of approval from the watching elders.

Lin Xiao's stomach tightened.

"Twenty-third formation!"

His turn.

He stepped onto the central platform, and the world narrowed to a single point—the summoning circle carved into the jade before him. It pulsed with a faint blue light, waiting for his qi to activate it. Behind him, he could feel the eyes of his fellow disciples. He knew what they were thinking. Lin Xiao, the boy from the Southern Slopes who'd barely passed his qi foundation exam. Lin Xiao, who'd never tamed so much as a field mouse.

*Prove them wrong*, he told himself.

He pressed his palm to the circle and poured his qi into it.

The light flared. The air crackled. And then—

Nothing.

The circle sputtered. Flickered. Died.

"Again," Elder Shen said, his voice flat.

Lin Xiao tried again. More qi this time, drawing from the deepest reserves of his core. The circle blazed to life, brighter than before, and shapes began to form within it. A serpent. Yes, a serpent—he could see the outline of its body, the curve of its fangs. His heart soared.

Then the light faded.

And the serpent that remained was nothing like the bronze-scaled beast he'd imagined.

It was barely two feet long. Its scales were a dull, sickly gray, cracked in a dozen places. One eye was clouded white. The other was the color of old blood. It lay curled in the center of the summoning circle, too weak to lift its head, and when it tried to hiss, only a pathetic wheeze escaped its throat.

Laughter rippled through the crowd.

"Is that... a wind-snake?"

"Looks like it's missing a few parts."

"Lin Xiao bonded with a cripple. What a joke."

Elder Shen's face was unreadable. "A spirit beast with a broken core," he said, his voice carrying no judgment but also no comfort. "It will never grow beyond its current stage. You may release the bond and attempt again in one year."

One year. A full year of humiliation, of watching his peers advance while he stagnated. A year of being the laughingstock of the valley.

Lin Xiao looked down at the snake.

It met his gaze with its one good eye. And in that eye, he saw something that made him pause. Not fear. Not pain. Something else. Something that looked almost like... recognition.

"Master Lin?" Elder Shen prompted. "Your decision?"

"I'll keep it."

The words left his mouth before he could stop them. The crowd's laughter turned to gasps, then to whispers. Even Elder Shen seemed taken aback.

"You understand what this means," the elder said slowly. "A broken-core beast cannot cultivate. It will never evolve. You will be bound to a companion that offers you no cultivation benefits, no battle prowess, no—"

"I understand." Lin Xiao knelt and extended his hand to the snake. "But I chose it. And it chose me."

The snake's tongue flickered out, touching his fingers. A spark of warmth traveled up his arm, and he felt the bond snap into place—a thread of silver light connecting his heart to the beast's. It was thin. Fragile. A bond that would never strengthen.

But it was his.

"Very well." Elder Shen turned away. "The ceremony continues. Next formation!"

Lin Xiao stepped off the platform, the snake coiled weakly around his wrist, and walked through a gauntlet of stares. Some were pitying. Most were mocking. A few—from the older disciples, the ones who'd been through this themselves—were simply curious.

*What kind of idiot keeps a broken beast?*

He didn't have an answer for them. He barely had an answer for himself.

---

The disciples' dormitory was a long stone building divided into small private rooms, each barely large enough for a bed and a desk. Lin Xiao's room was at the far end, furthest from the dining hall and closest to the latrines. A room for the lowest-ranked disciple.

He laid the snake on his bed and examined it properly for the first time.

Up close, it was worse than he'd thought. The cracks in its scales weren't just surface damage—they went deep, exposing the muscle beneath. The clouded eye was completely blind. And when he pressed his fingers gently against its body, he felt the broken core—a shattered crystal no bigger than a grain of rice, rattling loose inside its chest cavity.

"Great," he muttered. "I bonded with a corpse that hasn't had the decency to die yet."

The snake's tongue flickered. It seemed almost... amused.

"You can understand me, can't you?" Lin Xiao asked. Even a crippled wind-snake should have enough intelligence for basic communication. "Is there anything you can do? Anything at all?"

The snake stared at him.

Then, slowly, it opened its mouth.

And spoke.

"You have... interesting... qi."

Lin Xiao nearly fell off his bed. "You can talk?"

"All... spirit beasts can talk." The snake's voice was a whisper, each word separated by a long pause. "Most choose not to. Humans... talk too much."

"I've never heard of a wind-snake that can speak."

"Wind-snakes can't." The snake's eye glinted. "But I am not... a wind-snake."

Lin Xiao's heart stopped. "What do you mean?"

"What do you know... of the Thousand Beast Valley's... founding?"

The question caught him off guard. "I know it was established three thousand years ago by the Beast Saint, who tamed the first spirit beasts and taught humans how to bond with them."

"Good. What else?"

"He created the summoning circles. Established the laws of bonding. Wrote the—"

"He wrote nothing." The snake's voice grew stronger, each word less pained. "The Beast Saint's teachings were destroyed in the Purge Wars. Everything you know... is a reconstruction. Incomplete. Wrong."

Lin Xiao's mind raced. "How do you know this?"

"Because I was there."

The words hit him like a physical blow. "You're saying you're three thousand years old?"

"Older." The snake shifted, and for a moment, Lin Xiao saw something flicker beneath its dull scales—a pattern of light, like constellations swirling in a dark sky. "I was the Beast Saint's first bond. His most powerful. His... mistake."

"Mistake?"

"I was too strong. The other elders grew afraid. They conspired to destroy me." The snake's voice turned bitter. "They broke my core. Sealed me in a pocket dimension. Left me to rot for millennia... until your summoning circle cracked the seal."

Lin Xiao's head was spinning. "So you're... an ancient beast? A legendary creature?"

"Once. Now I am what you see. A crippled snake with a broken core and a few words left to speak before I fade entirely."

"And you bonded with me because...?"

"Because your qi resonated with mine." The snake's eye met his. "Because you saw me for what I am, not what I appear to be. And because..." It paused. "Because I have one last gift to give before I die."

"What gift?"

"Beast Resonance."

The words hung in the air like a promise. Or a curse.

"I've never heard of it," Lin Xiao said.

"Because it was forbidden. Erased from history. The Beast Saint created it, and the elders destroyed all records of it after they destroyed me." The snake's body began to glow, a faint silver light that pulsed in rhythm with Lin Xiao's heartbeat. "Beast Resonance allows a master and beast to merge their essences. To combine their powers. To become... more than the sum of their parts."

"You mean we can—"

"Fuse. Yes." The snake's voice was barely a whisper now. "But it comes at a cost. Each merge drains my remaining life force. I have perhaps... seven uses left. Seven chances for you to learn the technique before I am gone."

Lin Xiao felt tears prick at his eyes. "Seven?"

"Seven is enough." The snake's head drooped. "I have taught kings and tyrants, saints and sinners. But you... you kept me when others would have thrown me away. That is worth more than a thousand years of power."

"What do I do?"

"Close your eyes. Open your core. And listen."

Lin Xiao obeyed. He felt the snake's qi intertwine with his own, a thread of silver winding through the red-gold of his cultivation base. It was painful—like having his insides rearranged—but beneath the pain was something else. A warmth. A connection.

"Now," the snake whispered, "let me show you what it means to truly bond with a beast."

The world dissolved.

Lin Xiao found himself standing in a void of silver light. And in that void, he saw the snake—not as it was, but as it had been. A titan of scales and shadow, a creature that could swallow mountains and drink rivers dry. Its true form stretched across the void, infinite and terrible.

"This is what I was," the snake said. "And this is what you can become. If you dare."

"How?" Lin Xiao breathed.

"By mastering Beast Resonance. By merging with other beasts. By collecting their essences and making them your own." The snake's eye softened. "Your talent is rare, Lin Xiao. One in a billion. The Beast Saint himself only found three Resonators in his entire lifetime."

"And you'll teach me?"

"I will try." The snake's form began to fade. "But my time is short. You must find other beasts. Strong beasts. Bond with them. Learn their essences. And when the time comes, you must merge them all."

"Merge them all?"

"Seven beasts. Seven essences. Seven resonances." The snake's voice was fading. "That is the secret the elders tried to destroy. That is the path to true power."

Lin Xiao opened his eyes.

He was back in his dormitory, the snake coiled weakly on his chest. The silver light was gone. The warmth was fading
