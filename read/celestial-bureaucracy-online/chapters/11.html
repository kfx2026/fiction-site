Chapter 11: The Immortal's Secret Tab

~1.7k words · Updated 2026-06-22

Zhang Wei stared at the alert, his heart pounding against his ribs like a prisoner demanding escape. *Investigate and*—what? The notification cut off, leaving him dangling in uncertainty. He'd triggered something. That much was clear. But whether it was a simple system flag or a full-scale celestial manhunt, he had no way of knowing.

He closed the interface. The white void dissolved around him, and he found himself back in his cramped office cubicle, surrounded by stacks of destiny reports and the soft hum of divine machinery. The jade token sat on his desk, innocuous and silent, as if it hadn't just shown him the secrets of the universe.

"Zhang Wei!"

He jumped. Supervisor Hu stood in the doorway, his face a mask of controlled fury. Behind him, two figures in gold-trimmed robes stood at attention—Celestial Security, if Zhang had to guess. Their eyes glowed with the pale light of divine judgment, scanning the room as if looking for something hidden.

"Supervisor Hu," Zhang said, forcing his voice steady. "What a pleasant surprise."

"Don't play games with me." Hu stepped forward, his robes swishing against the floor. "The Core Administrative Interface registered an unauthorized access from your terminal. Explain yourself."

Zhang's mind raced. Denial was pointless—they had logs. Blaming it on a system error was possible, but risky. The truth was out of the question. That left only one option: misdirection.

"I was filing the overdue destiny reports," Zhang said, pointing at the stack on his desk. "You know, the ones you told me to prioritize. But the system kept throwing errors. Something about a corrupted index in the Reincarnation Queue."

Hu's eyes narrowed. "The Reincarnation Queue?"

"Yes. Specifically, Batch 374." Zhang leaned forward, lowering his voice conspiratorially. "I think someone's been tampering with the records. The system flagged an anomaly—a misrouted soul. But every time I tried to access the file, it redirected me to an error screen."

He watched Hu's face carefully. The supervisor's left eye twitched—barely noticeable, but Zhang caught it. *Bingo.*

"That's... concerning," Hu said slowly. "I'll have the technical division look into it."

"Should I continue filing the reports?"

"No." Hu turned to leave, then stopped. "Actually, yes. But stay away from the administrative interface. Consider it a direct order."

"Understood."

The Celestial Security guards followed Hu out, but one of them paused at the door. He was young—younger than Zhang, even—with a face that looked like it had never known a day of hardship. His eyes met Zhang's, and for a moment, something passed between them. Recognition? Curiosity? Zhang couldn't tell.

Then the guard was gone, and Zhang was alone.

He let out a breath he didn't realize he'd been holding. That was close. Too close. Hu knew something, but he wasn't ready to act on it yet. That gave Zhang time. Not much, but enough.

He pulled up the jade token again, careful to keep the interface minimized. The Core Administrative Interface was too risky to access directly now, but there were other tools at his disposal. Lesser functions, hidden in the bureaucracy's labyrinthine code.

*Karmic Ledger Viewing.*

The token pulsed, and a new window appeared. It was simpler than the sphere interface—just lines of text, names, and numbers. Debts and credits, measured in karmic units. Every action a soul took in life, recorded and quantified.

Zhang scrolled through the ledger, searching for familiar names. There was Li Xia, his former neighbor, who had a positive balance of 3,742 karma for feeding stray cats. There was the landlord who had evicted him, sitting at -8,912 karma for "habitual exploitation of vulnerable tenants." Good. That one would come due eventually.

And then he found it: *Hu Zhong, Supervisor, Department of Mortal Affairs.*

The number made Zhang's blood run cold.

*Karmic Balance: -247,891*

That wasn't just bad. That was catastrophic. For context, serial killers usually topped out at around -50,000. Genghis Khan had clocked in at -120,000. Hu had managed to accumulate nearly twice that, all while working in the *heavenly bureaucracy*.

Zhang scanned the entries, looking for the source. Most of it was standard corruption: falsified reports, suppressed errors, redirected karmic credit. But buried deep in the list was something else. A single line item that read:

*[REDACTED - Clearance Level: Tier 5 Required]*

Tier 5. That was Immortal rank. Supervisor Hu, a mid-level clerk, had accessed information meant for the highest echelons of celestial power. And whatever he'd done with it, it had cost him nearly a quarter million karma.

Zhang's finger hovered over the entry. Opening it would trigger another alert, of that he was certain. But the information inside could be the key to everything—to his death, to Hu's corruption, to the very nature of the heavenly bureaucracy itself.

He made a decision.

He tapped the entry.

The token screamed—a high-pitched whine that drilled into his skull. The interface flickered, warped, and then stabilized. A single message appeared:

[ACCESS GRANTED]
[User: Zhang Wei - ID 7845-B]
[Override: Temporary Tier 5 Clearance (1 Use Remaining)]

Below it, a file opened.

It was a video log, timestamped from three months before Zhang's death. The footage showed Hu standing in a darkened room, facing a figure shrouded in shadow. The figure spoke in a voice that sounded like grinding stones and breaking glass.

"Are you certain this will work?"

Hu nodded, his face slick with sweat. "The test subject is ideal. No family, no connections, no outstanding karmic debts. The system won't flag the loss."

"And the replacement?"

"Already prepared. A soul from the overflow queue, slated for recycling. I've redirected it to take the subject's place in the reincarnation cycle."

The shadowy figure leaned forward, and Zhang caught a glimpse of pale skin and eyes like dying stars. "Excellent. Then proceed. The Celestial Management System must be tested. If we can bypass its safeguards, the rest will fall into place."

"Yes, Master."

The footage ended.

Zhang stared at the blank screen, his mind reeling. *Test subject.* That was him. He hadn't died by accident. He'd been *selected*. Chosen as a guinea pig for some experiment designed to break the celestial system itself.

And Hu was just a pawn. The real mastermind was still out there, hiding in the shadows.

[SYSTEM MESSAGE: Unauthorized Access Detected]
[Source: Internal - ID 7845-B]
[Alert Level: Critical]
[Action: Celestial Security has been dispatched to your location]
[Estimated Time of Arrival: 3 Minutes]

Zhang cursed. Three minutes. He had three minutes to figure out what to do. He could run, but where would he go? The heavenly bureaucracy was infinite, but also inescapable. He could fight, but Celestial Security was armed with weapons that could erase a soul from existence.

Or he could do something they wouldn't expect.

He opened the jade token's interface one last time and typed a single command:

[Initiate: Emergency Protocol - Administrative Transfer]

A new window appeared:

[To: Department of Internal Affairs - Celestial Bureaucracy]
[From: Zhang Wei - ID 7845-B (Senior Clerk, Provisional)]
[Subject: Urgent Report of Malfeasance]

Zhang's fingers flew across the interface, copying the contents of the video log and Hu's karmic ledger into the message. He attached the original file, timestamp and all. Then he added a final line:

*I have evidence of a conspiracy that threatens the stability of the heavenly bureaucracy. My life is in danger. Please investigate immediately.*

He hit send.

The token went dark. A second later, the door to his office slammed open. Three Celestial Security officers stood in the doorway, their weapons drawn and glowing.

"Zhang Wei," the lead officer said, her voice flat and cold. "You are under arrest for unauthorized access to restricted celestial records. Any resistance will result in immediate soul deletion."

Zhang raised his hands slowly. "I understand. I'll cooperate."

The officer's eyes narrowed. "That's surprisingly reasonable."

"I've learned that fighting the system is pointless." Zhang smiled—a thin, humorless smile. "But I've also learned that the system has cracks. And cracks can be exploited."

He didn't resist as they bound his hands in chains of light. He didn't struggle as they led him out of the office and through the endless corridors of the heavenly bureaucracy. He just walked, counting the steps, memorizing the turns.

They passed other clerks, other departments. Some stared. Most pretended not to see. The bureaucracy was a machine, and machines didn't concern themselves with broken parts.

They reached a door made of black jade, carved with symbols that writhed and shifted when Zhang tried to focus on them.

"The Holding Cells," the officer said. "You'll remain here until your trial."

"And when is that?"

"Whenever the system schedules it."

Zhang nodded. That was fine. He had time. And while he waited, he would watch. He would listen. He would learn.

Because somewhere in this infinite, Kafkaesque nightmare of an afterlife, there was a man with eyes like dying stars who had killed him for an experiment. And Zhang Wei intended to return the favor.

The door opened, and he stepped into the darkness.

[SYSTEM MESSAGE: New Objective Unlocked]
[Objective: Survive Celestial Security Detention]
[Reward: Access to Hidden Faction - The Defragmented]
[Warning: This objective requires you to make contact with other dissidents within the system. Trust no one.]

The door slammed shut, plunging Zhang into absolute blackness.

But he wasn't alone.

A voice whispered from the shadows, soft and amused: "So. You're the one who broke into Hu's files."

Zhang tensed, his chains rattling. "Who's there?"

"Someone who's been waiting a long time for someone like you." A figure emerged from the darkness—a woman with silver hair and eyes that glowed like embers. She wore the robes of a Celestial Archivist, but they were torn and stained, as if she'd been in a fight. "I'm Lin Mei. And I think we have a mutual enemy."

She held out her hand.

Zhang looked at it, then at the darkness around them. Trust no one, the system had said. But the system was the one that had let him die.

He took her hand.

"Show me what you know."

Lin Mei smiled. "Gladly."

[CHAPTER END]
