CHAPTER 6: The Soul Forge Incident

The sphere's light dimmed as Zhang pulled his hand away, but the information remained seared into his mind. Supervisor Hu. Clerk ID 2319. The man who had personally processed Zhang's death—and then covered it up.

Zhang's jade token buzzed. A message appeared:

[URGENT: Department of Internal Auditing]
[Subject: Unauthorized System Access]
[Recipient: Zhang Wei - Clerk ID 7845-B]
[Body: Report to Floor 13, Room 1301 immediately. Failure to comply within 5 minutes will result in automatic disciplinary hearing.]

Five minutes. That wasn't enough time to think, let alone plan. Zhang's eyes darted to the glowing sphere. The Core Administrative Interface. It had given him power, but it had also made him visible. The system was watching him now.

He turned and ran.

The white void dissolved around him, and he found himself back in the hallway outside the filing room. The celestial marble floors stretched out before him, lined with identical doors and identical potted plants. Everything looked the same, but nothing felt the same. Zhang had seen the code beneath reality, and now he couldn't unsee it.

He sprinted down the corridor, his sandals slapping against the marble. Numbers flashed past him—room numbers, floor indicators, administrative zones. He needed somewhere to hide, somewhere to think.

Room 404. Storage Closet: Incense and Ritual Offerings.

Zhang skidded to a stop, threw open the door, and dove inside. The closet was cramped, filled with boxes of incense sticks, stacks of prayer paper, and jars of what looked like candied fruit. He pressed himself against the wall and tried to control his breathing.

His jade token pulsed again. Another message:

[TIME REMAINING: 4 minutes, 23 seconds]

"Great," Zhang muttered. "The system's timing me."

He pulled up the administrative panel he'd unlocked. The interface floated before his eyes, a translucent grid of numbers and symbols. Destiny Modification. Karmic Ledger Viewing. Timeline Adjustment. And the redacted one.

Zhang focused on Timeline Adjustment. A list of options appeared: [View Current Thread] [Create Branch] [Merge Threads] [Delete Thread]. That last one made him pause. Delete a timeline? That meant erasing someone's existence entirely. Even the thought of it felt wrong.

He selected [View Current Thread].

The world around him dissolved into a river of light. He saw himself—Zhang Wei, age 28, dead from a heart attack that never should have happened. But beyond that, he saw the threads that connected him to others. Supervisor Hu's thread was a thick, gnarled rope, tangled with hundreds of other souls. And there, at the edge of Zhang's vision, was a thread that glowed brighter than the rest.

Gold. Pure gold.

Zhang reached for it, and the thread wrapped around his finger. A name appeared: [REDACTED] - [REDACTED] - [Connection: Unknown].

"What the—"

The closet door burst open.

Zhang's concentration shattered, and the timeline vision vanished. Standing in the doorway was a woman in a silver robe, her hair pulled back in a severe bun. She carried a clipboard and wore an expression that could curdle milk.

"Zhang Wei," she said. "You are late."

"I was—"

"The Department of Internal Auditing does not accept excuses. Follow me."

Zhang stepped out of the closet, his mind racing. The woman studied him with cold, calculating eyes. She didn't look angry. She looked curious. That was worse.

They walked in silence through the corridors of the Heavenly Bureaucracy. Zhang tried to memorize the route, but the hallways twisted and turned in impossible ways. Left became right. Up became down. The building itself seemed to resist mapping.

Finally, they reached a door marked [Room 1301 - Internal Auditing Division].

The woman pushed it open, and Zhang stepped inside.

The room was a perfect cube, white on all sides. In the center sat a simple wooden desk. Behind the desk sat a man who looked like an accountant from a 1950s insurance commercial—round glasses, thinning hair, a tie that was slightly too tight.

"Sit," the man said.

Zhang sat.

The man folded his hands on the desk. "I am Auditor Chen. You accessed restricted records approximately seven minutes ago. Do you deny this?"

"No," Zhang said.

Auditor Chen's eyebrows rose. "No? You admit it?"

"Would lying help?"

"Unlikely."

"Then no."

Auditor Chen leaned back in his chair. "Most clerks, when confronted with evidence of their violations, attempt to lie. They claim system errors. They blame junior staff. They beg for mercy. You are... unusual."

"I've had a bad week."

"Indeed." Auditor Chen pulled a file from his desk. "Zhang Wei. Born 1995. Died 2023. Cause of death: cardiac arrest. Except..." He opened the file and tapped a line. "According to your medical records, you had no pre-existing heart conditions. Your blood pressure was normal. Your cholesterol was excellent. You died of a heart attack that, statistically speaking, should have been impossible."

Zhang's heart pounded. "I know."

"You know?"

"I accessed the records. My death was an administrative error. Someone in this department routed my soul to the wrong queue."

Auditor Chen's expression didn't change. "That is a serious accusation."

"It's not an accusation. It's a fact."

"Facts require evidence."

Zhang reached into his robe and pulled out his jade token. He held it up, letting the light catch the characters etched into its surface. "The Core Administrative Interface showed me the logs. My death was processed by Clerk ID 2319. That's Supervisor Hu."

For a long moment, Auditor Chen said nothing. Then he laughed.

It was not a friendly laugh.

"Zhang Wei," he said, wiping his eyes. "Do you have any idea who Supervisor Hu reports to?"

"No."

"Supervisor Hu reports to Section Chief Wang. Section Chief Wang reports to Deputy Director Li. Deputy Director Li reports to the Minister of Mortal Affairs herself. And the Minister..." Auditor Chen leaned forward. "The Minister is married to the Celestial Emperor's nephew."

Zhang's blood ran cold.

"You're telling me," Zhang said slowly, "that I can't report this because the person who killed me is connected to the Emperor?"

"I'm telling you that the system is designed to protect itself. Administrative errors happen. They are corrected quietly, without fanfare, and without blame. The alternative—public accountability—would destabilize the entire bureaucracy. Do you understand?"

"No."

Auditor Chen sighed. "Let me put it differently. You can pursue this complaint. You can demand justice. But to do so, you would need to accuse a Section Chief's subordinate of negligence. That Section Chief would then need to investigate. The investigation would reveal that the Deputy Director's office approved the error correction. The Deputy Director would then need to explain to the Minister why her department made a mistake. And the Minister would need to explain to the Emperor's family that her department killed a mortal by accident."

"So?"

"So the Emperor's family doesn't like bad news. They would fire the Minister. The Minister would fire the Deputy Director. The Deputy Director would fire the Section Chief. And the Section Chief—" Auditor Chen smiled thinly. "—would fire Supervisor Hu. But before that happens, every single person in that chain of command would have a very strong incentive to make sure the initial complaint never reached them."

Zhang stared at him. "You're telling me that if I report this, I'll be killed. Again."

"No. If you report this, you will be erased. There's a difference. Death implies an afterlife. Erasure implies nothing."

The weight of Auditor Chen's words settled over Zhang like a shroud. He had thought he was playing a game. He had thought he could hack the system, expose the truth, and achieve immortality. But the system wasn't a machine. It was a living, breathing network of relationships and power. And he was a single thread, easily cut.

"I see," Zhang said quietly.

"Do you?"

"Yes." Zhang stood up. "Thank you for your time, Auditor Chen."

"Sit down."

Zhang hesitated. Auditor Chen's voice had changed. It was no longer friendly. It was no longer threatening. It was... interested.

"I said sit."

Zhang sat.

Auditor Chen removed his glasses and cleaned them with a cloth. "I told you all of that for a reason, Zhang Wei. Not to discourage you. To test you."

"Test me?"

"I have been an auditor for three thousand years. In that time, I have processed exactly seventeen complaints of administrative error. Sixteen of those complainants chose to accept compensation and move on. One chose to pursue the truth."

"What happened to them?"

"Which one?"

"All of them."

Auditor Chen smiled. "The sixteen who accepted compensation now hold comfortable positions in the bureaucracy. They have good jobs, good benefits, good afterlives. The one who pursued the truth..."

He paused, letting the silence stretch.

"She became my superior."

Zhang's jaw dropped. "What?"

"That woman who brought you here? She was the complainant. Seventeen hundred years ago, she discovered that her death was an accident. She chose to fight. She lost everything—her position, her status, her soul. She was consigned to the Hell of Unending Data Entry. But she didn't stop fighting. She learned the system. She mastered it. And eventually, she broke it."

"She's the head of Internal Auditing?"

"She's the head of everything." Auditor Chen's eyes glinted. "The woman you met is not just my superior. She is the second most powerful being in the Heavenly Bureaucracy. She answers only to the Celestial Emperor himself."

Zhang's mind reeled. The woman with the severe bun and the clipboard. The woman who had looked at him like he was a puzzle to be solved. She had started exactly where he was now.

"She wants to meet you," Auditor Chen said. "Properly. Tomorrow, at the Hour of the Ox. The Soul Forge."

"The Soul Forge?"

"You'll find out when you get there." Auditor Chen returned his glasses to his face. "One piece of advice: don't be late. She hates lateness."

Zhang nodded slowly. His jade token pulsed.

[SYSTEM MESSAGE: New Quest Available]
[Title: The Soul Forge Incident]
[Objective: Meet with [REDACTED] at the Soul Forge]
[Reward: Unknown]
[Penalty for Failure: Permanent Soul Erasure]

Permanent soul erasure. That was new.

Zhang stood, his legs shaky. As he reached the door, Auditor Chen spoke again.

"Oh, and Zhang Wei? One more thing."

"What?"

"The gold thread you saw. The bright one. I wouldn't recommend following it."

Zhang froze. "How did you—"

"I told you. I've been an auditor for three thousand years." Auditor Chen smiled. "I see everything."

Zhang left the room with his heart pounding and his mind racing. The gold thread. The woman in silver. The Soul Forge. Everything was connected, but he couldn't see how.

He walked down the corridor, past the identical doors and identical potted plants, until he reached a window. Outside, the sky of the celestial realm stretched endlessly, a tapestry of stars and clouds.

Somewhere out there was the truth.

And Zhang Wei, Junior Clerk, Department of Mortal Affairs, was going to find it.

Even if it killed him.

Again.

His jade token buzzed one final time:

[REMINDER: Hour of the Ox - 3:00 AM Celestial Standard Time]
[Location: Sector 7 - The Soul Forge
