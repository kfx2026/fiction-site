CHAPTER 16: The Protocol That Shouldn't Exist

Zhang barely had time to process the alert before the sphere in front of him flickered. Warning symbols bloomed across its surface like bruises, red characters bleeding into the space around him.

[UNAUTHORIZED ACCESS DETECTED]
[INITIATING COUNTERMEASURES]
[ESTIMATED TIME UNTIL SYSTEM PURGE: 47 SECONDS]

"Of course," Zhang muttered, already moving. He didn't know what a system purge looked like, but he had a strong suspicion it wasn't the kind you survived. His fingers flew across the interface, calling up the commands he'd memorized from the forbidden database. "Cancel countermeasures. Authorization: Zhang Wei, ID 7845-B, Senior Clerk Provisional."

[INVALID AUTHORIZATION]

"Override with emergency protocol."

[INVALID PROTOCOL]

"Override with—" Zhang stopped. The sphere was pulsing faster now, the red consuming more of its surface. He had seconds left. And then he remembered something. Something he'd seen in the database but dismissed because it made no sense.

He typed: [PROTOCOL 000: HEAVENLY RESET]

The sphere went dark.

For three agonizing seconds, Zhang floated in absolute silence and blackness. The kind of silence that pressed against your ears like physical weight. The kind of darkness that made you question whether you still had eyes.

Then the sphere lit up again, but different. The gears now spun in reverse. The runes glowed with a soft blue light instead of harsh gold. And a new message appeared:

[PROTOCOL 000 ACCEPTED]
[SYSTEM RUNNING IN LEGACY MODE]
[TRACE PROTOCOLS: DISABLED]
[ALERT SYSTEM: OFFLINE]
[Welcome back, Administrator]

Administrator.

The word hit Zhang like a physical blow. He stared at it, waiting for the punchline, waiting for another notification to correct the "mistake." But nothing came.

He was now an Administrator.

Not a Junior Clerk. Not a Senior Clerk. Not even a Supervisor or Department Head. An Administrator. The kind of being that made gods nervous.

"How?" Zhang whispered.

The sphere pulsed once, and a new interface appeared. It was different from the bureaucratic nightmare he'd been navigating. This one was clean, intuitive, almost friendly. It showed the entire structure of the Celestial Bureaucracy as a series of interconnected nodes, each one representing a department, a function, a god.

And at the very top, above even the Celestial Emperor, was a single node labeled:

[ORIGINAL ADMINISTRATOR]
STATUS: VACANT

Zhang's blood ran cold. Original Administrator. Vacant. As in, the position was empty. As in, someone had been there once, and now they weren't.

The sphere pulsed again, and more data flowed into his mind. The truth. The real truth behind the Celestial Bureaucracy.

It hadn't always been this way. Once, the system was simple. Souls lived, souls died, souls reincarnated. No karma points. No destiny reports. No audits. Just a clean cycle of existence.

Then the Original Administrator disappeared.

And the gods, left without guidance, built their own system. Layer upon layer of rules, regulations, and redundancies. They created the bureaucracy to fill the void left by their absent creator. But they didn't understand the original code. They just kept adding patches and workarounds, building a tower of celestial red tape that grew more unstable with each passing millennium.

The "bugs" Zhang had found weren't bugs. They were features of the original system, buried under centuries of bureaucratic garbage.

And now the system had recognized him as an Administrator. Why? Because he'd accessed the original protocol. Because he'd proven he could see through the layers of obfuscation. Because—

A new notification appeared. This one wasn't from the system. It was from the jade token, which was now glowing with frantic urgency.

[URGENT MESSAGE - Supervisor Hu]
[Zhang. My office. NOW.]

Zhang closed the sphere interface. The white void dissolved around him, and he found himself back at his desk, the jade token still warm in his hand. Li Feng was staring at him with wide eyes.

"Where did you just go?" she asked. "You were gone for fifteen minutes. I was about to report you missing."

"Went to the bathroom," Zhang said.

"Your body was still here."

Damn.

"Meditation technique," Zhang said. "Very advanced. You wouldn't understand."

Li Feng's eyes narrowed, but before she could press further, a shadow fell over them both. Supervisor Hu stood there, his face a mask of barely controlled fury.

"Office. Now." He didn't wait for a response, just turned and walked away.

Zhang followed.

Hu's office was exactly what you'd expect from a mid-level celestial bureaucrat: functional, drab, and covered in motivational posters that said things like "SYNERGY IS KEY" and "REPORT ERRORS PROMPTLY." The only personal touch was a small jade statue of a three-legged toad sitting on a pile of gold coins.

"Sit," Hu said, gesturing to a chair.

Zhang sat.

Hu circled around his desk, not sitting himself. He just stood there, staring at Zhang with an intensity that would have made lesser men sweat. Zhang had dealt with worse managers. Once, his boss in life had made him work through Christmas while she went on a cruise. Hu's silent treatment was amateur hour.

"I've been reviewing your file," Hu said finally. "Interesting reading. Went from entry-level clerk to Senior Clerk Provisional in less than a week. That's... unprecedented."

"I'm a fast learner."

"Indeed." Hu picked up a scroll from his desk. "And you've also accessed seventeen restricted databases, modified twenty-three destiny threads, and somehow convinced the celestial ledger to show a zero balance for your entire department's karmic deficit."

Zhang kept his face neutral. "I don't know what you're talking about."

"Don't." Hu slammed the scroll down. "Don't play stupid with me. I've been in this department for three thousand years. I know when someone is gaming the system. And you, Zhang Wei, are gaming the system."

"Prove it."

Hu's eye twitched. "I don't need to prove it. I just need to report it. And I have. The Celestial Compliance Division is opening an investigation. They'll be here within the hour."

Now Zhang felt a chill. Celestial Compliance Division. He'd seen references to them in the database. They were the immortals you called when you wanted someone to stop existing. Not just die. Stop existing, permanently, across all timelines.

"They won't find anything," Zhang said.

"Oh, I'm sure they won't. You're clearly competent enough to cover your tracks." Hu's smile was thin and predatory. "But the investigation itself will take time. And resources. And during that time, your access will be revoked. Your provisional status will be suspended. And you'll be reassigned to the department of your... predecessor."

"Predecessor?"

Hu picked up a small token from his desk. It was identical to the one Zhang had received on his first day, except this one was cracked down the middle. "The last clerk who tried what you're trying. Clerk ID 7844. Your predecessor."

Zhang's mind raced. "What happened to him?"

"He was reassigned." Hu's smile widened. "To the Department of Non-Existent Souls. It's a very small department. Only one employee. And the employee doesn't exist."

"You're lying."

"Am I?" Hu tossed the token to Zhang. "Check for yourself. Access the personnel records. If you still can."

Zhang caught the token. It was cold in his hand, colder than it should have been. He focused his will, trying to access the system, but something was blocking him. The interface that had been so clear just minutes ago was now fuzzy, unresponsive.

[ACCESS RESTRICTED]
[USER: ZHANG WEI]
[STATUS: PENDING INVESTIGATION]
[ALL PRIVILEGES: SUSPENDED]

Zhang looked up at Hu. The supervisor was watching him with the satisfied expression of a cat that had cornered a mouse.

"You see," Hu said, "I've been doing this for three thousand years. I've seen a hundred ambitious clerks try to climb the ladder. And I've seen every single one of them fall. The Celestial Bureaucracy doesn't reward ambition. It rewards patience. It rewards conformity. And most of all, it rewards loyalty."

"Loyalty to what?" Zhang asked. "A system that's broken? A system that kills people because of clerical errors and then covers it up?"

Hu's expression hardened. "Loyalty to the system itself. The system is perfect. The system is eternal. The system is the will of Heaven made manifest. And you, Zhang Wei, have been trying to hack it. Which means you are a threat."

"I found a way to fix it."

"You found a way to break it." Hu leaned forward, his face inches from Zhang's. "And now you'll be unmade. That's the price of ambition in Heaven."

Zhang stared into Hu's eyes. He could see the truth there. Hu wasn't just a bureaucrat. He was a gatekeeper. A guardian of the status quo. And he'd done this before. Probably many times.

But Hu had made one mistake.

He'd given Zhang the cracked token.

Even with his access suspended, Zhang could still feel the faint pulse of Administrator-level code running through his soul. The protocol he'd activated had changed him at a fundamental level. The system might have locked his user account, but it couldn't lock what he'd become.

Zhang smiled.

"What's so funny?" Hu asked.

"Nothing," Zhang said. "I was just thinking about how long it took you to notice something."

"Notice what?"

"That I've already done more damage than you can imagine."

Hu's eyes widened. He turned to his desk, frantically pulling up interfaces. "What did you do? What did you—"

The first explosion shook the office.

It wasn't a physical explosion. It was a karmic explosion. A sudden, violent release of accumulated spiritual debt that rippled through the Celestial Bureaucracy like a shockwave. Alarms began wailing. Red lights flashed.

[SYSTEM ALERT]
[MASSIVE KARMIC IMBALANCE DETECTED]
[SOURCE: DEPARTMENT OF MORTAL AFFAIRS - DIVISION 7]
[MULTIPLE DESTINY THREADS: CORRUPTED]
[RECOMMENDED ACTION: EVACUATE]

"What have you done?" Hu screamed.

Zhang stood up slowly. "I didn't do anything. The system did it. Or rather, the system finally showed what's been there all along."

He reached for his jade token. It was still locked, still restricted. But now he could feel something new. A connection. A thread leading from his token to the core of the system itself.

The Protocol 000 activation hadn't just given him Administrator access. It had made him part of the original code. A living key to the system's true functions.

And that meant he could unlock anything.

Zhang focused, and the token flared to life. Not with the golden light of the bureaucracy, but with the blue light of the original system. The light of the missing Administrator.

[ACCESS RESTORED]
[USER: ZHANG WEI]
[STATUS: ADMINISTRATOR (ACTING)]
[ALL PRIVILEGES: UNLOCKED]

Hu stumbled backward, his face pale. "That's not possible. That's not—"

"It is possible," Zhang said.
