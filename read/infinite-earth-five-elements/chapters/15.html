CHAPTER 15: The Weight of Seventeen Seconds

Ren's knees buckled.

The golden light that had wrapped his body like a second skin flickered and died. His right arm—the one that had pulsed with that impossible radiance—now hung limp at his side, the cuts from the hounds' claws weeping blood onto the cracked stone.

*Seventeen seconds.*

That was how long the system's black void had lasted. Seventeen seconds of absolute nothing. Seventeen seconds of staring into a darkness so complete it felt like the universe had forgotten to exist.

But it hadn't been seventeen seconds for him.

It had been *hours*.

"Ren!" Lin Wei was at his side before he hit the ground, her hands gripping his shoulders, her face pale in the dim light of Mei's rekindled flame. "Your eyes—they went *white*—"

"I'm fine." The words came out wrong. Too deep. Too resonant. Like three different people speaking at once. Ren coughed, tasted copper, and tried again. "I'm fine. Just—give me a second."

Chen Feng approached cautiously, his rebar clutched like a lifeline. The man who had been ready to fight Ren an hour ago now looked at him like he was something sacred. Something dangerous.

"What happened?" Chen Feng's voice cracked. "One second you're covered in that light, the next you're staring at nothing, and then—" He pointed at the corpses. "—they all started *rotting*."

Ren looked.

The hounds' bodies were dissolving. Not like normal decay. Faster. The flesh sloughed off bone in sheets, the bones themselves crumbling to dust, the dust sinking into the cracks of the temple plaza as if the earth was hungry for them.

*He was draining them.*

The thought came unbidden. He hadn't done it consciously. But in that seventeen-second void, while his body stood frozen in the real world, he had *learned* something. The system had shown him something.

*Five Element Resonance isn't a combat skill.*

The memory surfaced like a bubble from deep water. In that endless dark, he had seen diagrams. Charts. A lattice of connections that spanned dimensions he didn't have words for. The elements weren't just forces to be bent. They were *threads*. And every living thing was woven from them.

*Five Element Resonance is a harvesting technique.*

The system had designed it as a way to reclaim elemental energy from defeated enemies. A way to grow stronger without waiting for natural accumulation. A way to—

*Skip the grind.*

Ren's stomach turned.

"Your arm," Lin Wei whispered.

He looked down. The cuts were closing. Not healing—*closing*. The flesh knitted itself together with audible pops, the blood retreating back into his veins like time had reversed. But beneath the skin, something moved. Something that made his palm itch and his fingers twitch.

*The hounds' elements. I absorbed them.*

Seventeen kills. All of them corrupted creatures of the wild, twisted by the system's energy release. Each one carried traces of the elements they had consumed. And Ren had taken all of it.

**[Elemental Reserves: Overflowing]**
**Warning: Unstable Equilibrium**
**Recommendation: Cycle elements before forced release.**

"Forced release?" Ren muttered. "What does that mean?"

The system didn't answer.

But his body did.

A spike of heat shot up his spine, followed immediately by a cold so intense his teeth chattered. His left hand burst into flame—actual, visible flame—while his right hand frosted over with ice crystals that spread up his forearm.

"Get back!" Mei shouted, her own fire flaring in response. "His elements are destabilizing!"

*She can feel it.*

Of course she could. Mei was a Fire specialist. She had attuned herself to a single element, and now she could sense the chaos raging inside him. The imbalance. The *wrongness* of a body trying to contain five warring forces at once.

Ren dropped to his knees.

*I need to cycle them.*

The knowledge was there, buried in the system's data dump. A technique. A breathing pattern. A way to move energy through the body in a specific sequence that would stabilize the five elements and merge them into something greater.

*True Qi.*

The system had given him seventy-one hours. That was all the time he had before the Administrator arrived. Failure meant permanent deletion. Success meant—

The technique surfaced in his mind.

*Breathe in. Draw from Earth. Cycle to Water. Convert to Wood. Ignite with Fire. Forge with Metal. Return to Earth.*

Simple. Elegant. Impossible.

Because the first step required him to draw from *Earth*, and the Earth was one hundred meters below the floating island he stood on. The element was there, yes. But distant. Filtered through the stone and soil that had been ripped from the planet's crust.

And his reserves were already overflowing.

"Ren, you need to do something." Lin Wei's voice was tight with fear. "Your skin is *cracking*."

He looked at his hands. She was right. Fine lines had appeared on his palms, and through them, light leaked out. Golden light. The same light that had marked his awakening.

*I'm going to explode.*

The realization hit with cold clarity. His body couldn't contain the energy. The seventeen-second void had given him knowledge, but it had also accelerated his absorption. He had taken in the elements of seventeen corrupted beasts, and now his system was screaming for release.

"Everyone get back." Ren's voice was steadier than he felt. "Far back. Behind the temple walls."

"Like hell—" Chen Feng started.

"*Now.*"

The command carried weight. Literally. The air around Ren rippled, and the survivors stumbled backward as if pushed by an invisible hand. The Earth element. He had spoken with the authority of stone and bedrock, and their bodies had responded.

Lin Wei was the last to go. Her eyes met his, and for a moment, he saw the question she couldn't ask.

*Are you going to survive this?*

He didn't have an answer.

She turned and ran.

Ren closed his eyes.

*Breathe in.*

The air burned. Not with heat, but with *presence*. Every molecule carried fragments of element, pieces of the shattered world that had reorganized itself into the current reality. He drew them in, felt them settle in his lungs, and began the cycle.

*Draw from Earth.*

The island groaned. Deep beneath the temple, the stone that had been ripped from the planet's crust answered his call. Energy flowed upward through his legs, into his core, filling the reservoir that had been depleted by the hounds' attack.

*Cycle to Water.*

The moisture in the air responded. The blood in his veins. The fluids in every cell of his body. He pulled them together, directed them, forced them to align with the Earth energy in a spiral that wound through his dantian.

*Convert to Wood.*

This was the hard part. Wood didn't exist in pure form on this island. It was derived from Earth and Water, a combination that required precise control. Ren focused, imagined the energy changing state, becoming something green and living and growing.

His fingers tingled. Tiny sprouts emerged from his skin, curling around his knuckles before withering and dying. The Wood element was trying to manifest, but his body wasn't designed for it.

*Keep going.*

*Ignite with Fire.*

The sprouts caught flame. Pain lanced through his hands as the fire consumed the dying wood, but Ren didn't stop. He channeled the heat, directed it, used it to superheat the energy flowing through his meridians.

*Forge with Metal.*

This was where the Golden light came from. Metal was the element of structure, of permanence, of the immutable laws that governed reality. Ren pulled the fire-heated energy and slammed it into a Metal framework, creating a vessel that could contain the chaos.

*Return to Earth.*

Complete the cycle. Bring the energy back to its source, purified and stabilized. Let the Earth absorb what it had given, enriched by the journey through the other elements.

Ren exhaled.

The golden light faded. The cracks in his skin sealed. The flame on his left hand died, and the ice on his right hand melted.

He was stable.

For now.

**[Elemental Equilibrium: Restored]**
**Cycle Efficiency: 23%**
**Warning: Suboptimal. Practice required.**
**True Qi Progress: 0.7%**

Zero point seven percent.

Seventy-one hours to reach one hundred percent.

Ren opened his eyes.

The temple plaza was empty. The hounds' bodies had fully dissolved, leaving only dark stains on the stone. The survivors were huddled behind the temple wall, peeking around the corner with expressions ranging from terror to awe.

Lin Wei broke from the group and ran toward him.

"You're okay." It wasn't a question. She grabbed his face, turned his head left and right, checked his eyes. "You're actually okay."

"For now." Ren stood, feeling the newfound stability in his limbs. "But I need to understand what happened. The system said I have seventy-one hours before—"

**SYSTEM ALERT**

The notification appeared in his vision, but it was different this time. The text was red. Pulsing. Urgent.

**Administrator response time accelerated.**
**Anomaly detected in host's elemental signature.**
**Countermeasure initiated.**
**Estimated arrival: 17 hours, 23 minutes, 41 seconds.**

"Seventeen hours?" Ren's blood ran cold. "That's—that's not enough time—"

**Additional alert:**
**Elemental Convergence detected.**
**A being of similar nature has been identified.**
**Location: 2.3 kilometers north-northwest.**
**Recommendation: Locate and eliminate before convergence triggers cascade failure.**

Lin Wei read the notification over his shoulder. Her face went pale.

"There's another one? Someone else with multiple elements?"

Ren looked north. Past the temple ruins, past the shattered buildings of what used to be a university campus, past the edge of the floating island itself. Two point three kilometers. On a fragment of Earth that was barely five kilometers across.

Someone was out there.

Someone like him.

And the system wanted them dead.

"Ren." Lin Wei's hand found his. "What are you going to do?"

He looked at the survivors. Thirty-seven people, huddled in the ruins of a temple, trying to survive in a world that had turned into a nightmare. They looked to him now. He could see it in their eyes. He was their hope.

But hope wasn't enough.

Not against an Administrator.

Not against a convergence.

Not against seventeen hours.

Ren squeezed Lin Wei's hand, then let go.

"I'm going to find them."

He took a step toward the north edge of the island.

And the ground beneath his feet *exploded*.
