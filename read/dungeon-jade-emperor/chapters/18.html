CHAPTER 18: The Seventh Floor's Secret

Wei Chen stood at the entrance of the seventh floor, his hand pressed against the newly formed barrier. Three weeks had passed since the first cultivators entered his dungeon. Three weeks of constant refinement, constant battle, constant growth.

And now something was wrong.

The barrier hummed beneath his palm, but the vibration was off—uneven, like a heart with a stutter. He'd felt it the moment he woke up that morning, a discordant note in the dungeon's normally harmonious song.

"What's happening to you?" he murmured.

The core pulsed weakly. *Something... changed. Below.*

"Below? There's nothing below. The sixth floor is the deepest."

*No. Deeper.*

Wei Chen's blood ran cold. The dungeon had seven floors. He'd built six. The seventh was supposed to be empty, a placeholder for future expansion. But the core was telling him something had taken root there.

Something he hadn't planted.

He pushed through the barrier, and the temperature dropped twenty degrees. The air grew thick, heavy with a presence that felt nothing like the pure qi of the Heavenly Court. It was cold. Hungry.

The seventh floor was supposed to be bare stone, a blank canvas waiting for his designs. But as Wei Chen descended the spiraling stairs, he saw light—pale, sickly green, pulsing like a wound.

The stairs opened into a cavern he didn't recognize. The walls were slick with moisture, covered in veins of dark crystal that throbbed with each pulse of the green light. In the center of the floor, a fissure had opened in the stone, and from that fissure, something was growing.

It looked like a heart. A massive, crystalline heart, easily the size of a carriage, embedded in the floor with tendrils spreading outward like roots. Each tendril burrowed into the stone, drinking from the dungeon's qi, corrupting everything it touched.

"What the hell is that?" Wei Chen whispered.

The core's voice was faint, distant. *Corruption. From the void between realms. It has... found us.*

"Found us? How? I've sealed every entrance, every crack—"

*It was already here. Sleeping. Waiting.*

Wei Chen's mind raced. The old Heavenly Court hadn't just fallen—it had been poisoned. Infiltrated. Something had been planted in its depths, something that had waited ten thousand years for a new host.

And he'd walked right into the trap.

He approached the heart cautiously, his qi flaring around him like a protective shield. The corruption in the air made his skin crawl, made his thoughts feel sluggish and wrong. He had to focus, had to push through the mental fog.

The heart pulsed. Once. Twice. And then it spoke.

*Wei Chen.*

The voice was ancient, vast, and utterly inhuman. It resonated in his bones, in his soul, in places he'd never known existed.

"You know my name."

*We have been watching. Since you first touched the core. Since you first breathed life into this corpse of a court.*

"You're the reason the Heavenly Court fell."

*We are the reason all things fall. Time. Empires. Gods. We are the end that waits at the end of all things.*

Wei Chen's hand went to his storage ring. He had talismans, formations, a dozen different weapons. But something told him none of them would work against this. This wasn't an enemy he could fight with brute force.

"What do you want?"

*We want what we have always wanted. Completion. The Heavenly Court was the last bastion of order in a universe that should be chaos. We consumed it, but its core escaped. Now the core has chosen a new master. A weak one.*

"I'm not weak."

*You are a tomb raider. A thief. A man who has spent his life stealing from the dead. You think that makes you strong? You have not even begun to understand the power you hold.*

The heart pulsed again, and the tendrils spread further, creeping up the walls, reaching toward the ceiling. Wei Chen could feel them drinking the dungeon's qi, draining it like a parasite.

"I'll destroy you."

*You cannot destroy us. We are not a thing to be destroyed. We are a principle. An inevitability. But you can... delay us. If you are clever.*

"And why would I do that? Why would I help something that wants to consume everything?"

*Because we offer you a choice. Serve us, and we will let your dungeon live. Resist us, and we will consume it from within. Your choice, Wei Chen. Serve or die.*

The voice faded, and the heart fell silent, pulsing gently like a sleeping beast.

Wei Chen stood there for a long moment, his mind churning. The corruption was already spreading. If he didn't do something, it would consume the seventh floor, then the sixth, then all the way up to the entrance. His dungeon would become a nest of void corruption, a blight on the world.

But he couldn't serve it. He wouldn't.

He turned and climbed back up the stairs, his steps heavy. The core pulsed weakly as he emerged into the sixth floor, and he felt its pain, its fear. It was dying. The corruption was killing it.

"Not today," Wei Chen muttered. "Not on my watch."

He spent the next hour in the meditation room, his mind reaching out to the dungeon, feeling every crack and crevice, every vein of qi. The corruption was concentrated in the seventh floor, but its tendrils were spreading upward, slowly, inexorably.

He couldn't cut them out. They were too deep, too integrated with the dungeon's structure. But maybe he could redirect them. Bury them. Starve them of the qi they needed to grow.

It was a desperate plan, born of desperation. But it was all he had.

He began to work. He carved new formation arrays into the walls of the sixth floor, designed to divert the flow of qi away from the corruption. He reinforced the barrier between the sixth and seventh floors, layering it with purification talismans. He poured his own qi into the core, feeding it strength to fight the invasion.

Hours passed. His hands bled. His vision blurred. But he didn't stop.

By the time he finished, the corruption had been contained. Not destroyed—he couldn't destroy it—but contained. The seventh floor was sealed, isolated from the rest of the dungeon. The heart would continue to grow, but it would grow slowly, fed only by the ambient qi it could leech from the void.

It was a temporary solution. A bandage on a wound that needed surgery.

But it would have to do.

Wei Chen collapsed against the wall of the audience hall, exhausted. The core pulsed weakly, gratefully, sending a wave of warmth through his body.

"That thing," he said, his voice hoarse. "It's been there since the beginning?"

*Since before the beginning. It was the seed of the Heavenly Court's destruction, planted by an enemy whose name has been lost to time.*

"An enemy? Who?"

*We do not know. The memory is corrupted. But we know this: they are still out there. Watching. Waiting. They will return when the heart has grown strong enough.*

Wei Chen closed his eyes. The weight of ten thousand years pressed down on him, the burden of a fallen empire, of a war that had never truly ended.

"I didn't ask for this," he whispered.

*No one asks for destiny. They simply... survive it.*

He opened his eyes. The audience hall was quiet, the golden light of the murals casting long shadows across the jade floor. Guardian One stood at attention in the corner, its blank face staring into nothing.

Tomorrow, cultivators would come. They would challenge his floors, test his traps, fight his guardians. They would grow stronger, and so would he.

And somewhere, deep beneath the earth, a heart of corruption pulsed, waiting for its master to return.

Wei Chen pushed himself to his feet. He was tired. Broken. Terrified.

But he was not beaten.

"We're going to need more guardians," he said. "And more floors. And a way to turn that heart into a weapon."

The core pulsed. *You cannot corrupt the corruption.*

"Watch me."

He walked to the center of the hall and sat down, crossing his legs. The core's energy flowed into him, filling him with strength and knowledge. He reached out with his mind, feeling the dungeon's every fiber, every vein of qi.

And he began to plan.

The heart was a problem. But problems could be solved. And if he couldn't destroy it, he would find a way to use it. To turn the enemy's weapon into his own.

It was a dangerous game. The kind of game that got people killed.

But Wei Chen had been playing dangerous games his whole life. And he was very, very good at them.

As he sank into meditation, the core pulsed one last time, a whisper of warning.

*They are coming. The void's servants. They have felt the heart's awakening.*

"How long?"

*Days. Perhaps hours.*

Wei Chen's eyes snapped open. He rose to his feet, his exhaustion forgotten, replaced by a cold, clear focus.

"Then we don't have time to waste."

He strode toward the stairs, his mind already racing with designs, formations, traps. The void was coming. The enemy was coming.

And the new Jade Emperor would be waiting.
