Chapter 5: The Beast's Awakening

~1.9k words · Updated 2026-06-22

The mountain path blurred before Shen He's eyes. Each step sent jolts of pain through his battered body, but he kept moving, kept walking, kept putting distance between himself and the only home he'd ever known.

The sun hung low in the sky, painting the clouds in shades of amber and crimson. In another life, he might have stopped to appreciate the view. Now, it only reminded him of all he'd lost.

*Three years.* Three years of scrubbing floors, organizing scrolls, and enduring the contempt of every disciple who crossed his path. Three years of sneaking into the restricted section, reading by moonlight, memorizing formulas that could reshape his destiny. Three years of hoping that if he just worked hard enough, learned enough, sacrificed enough—someone would see his worth.

Instead, they'd seen a thief.

Shen He's hand drifted to his pocket, where the crumpled page of notes rested against his thigh. The Midnight Lotus and Phoenix Tail Grass. The ratios were burned into his memory now, etched there by the explosion that had nearly killed him. But the third ingredient—the one he'd been searching for when Lin Xue caught him—remained a mystery.

A stone turned beneath his foot, and he stumbled, catching himself against a gnarled tree trunk. The bark scraped his palm, drawing blood.

"Watch where you're going, cripple."

The voice came from above. Shen He looked up to find three figures blocking the path ahead. Outer disciples. Young, arrogant, their cultivation bases barely above the first stage of Qi Condensation. But to Shen He, they might as well have been immortals.

"Step aside," he said, his voice flat.

"Or what?" The leader, a stocky boy with a scar above his left eyebrow, stepped forward. "You'll throw another exploding pill at us? I heard about what happened. Nearly took out half the compound. The elders said you were dangerous."

"They said I was exiled."

"Same thing." The boy's companions laughed. "A cripple who can't cultivate, playing with fire. It's almost sad, really. Did you really think you could use alchemy to fix your broken meridians? That's like trying to fill a sieve with water."

Shen He's hands curled into fists. "I said step aside."

"Make us."

They spread out, forming a loose semicircle around him. The leader cracked his knuckles, a lazy smile spreading across his face. "You know, I've always wondered what it would feel like to hit someone who can't hit back. The elders always said we shouldn't bully the weak, but you're not a disciple anymore, are you? You're just a vagrant."

Shen He's mind raced. He had no pills left, no weapons, no cultivation base to defend himself. The three disciples were nothing special, but they were still miles beyond him in raw power.

*Use what you have,* a voice whispered in his mind. *You're not helpless. You're just underestimated.*

He reached into his pocket and pulled out his notes, holding them up like a shield. "You want to know why I was really exiled?"

The leader paused, curiosity flickering in his eyes. "What are you talking about?"

"This." Shen He waved the paper. "The Meridian Restoration Pill. The formula the sect has hidden for centuries. I found it. I decoded it. And I figured out something they didn't want anyone to know."

"You're lying."

"Am I?" Shen He's voice dropped, taking on a conspiratorial tone. "Why do you think Elder Wu was so eager to have me thrown out? Why do you think Lin Xue herself came to watch my trial? They're scared. Scared that someone like me—a cripple, a nobody—might have unlocked a secret they've been guarding for generations."

The disciples exchanged uncertain glances. The leader's bravado wavered.

"Give me the paper," he said, holding out his hand.

Shen He smiled. It was not a friendly smile. "Come and take it."

The leader lunged.

Shen He didn't try to dodge. Instead, he dropped to his knees, letting the boy's momentum carry him forward. As the disciple stumbled past, Shen He grabbed a handful of dust and threw it into his eyes.

"AHH! MY EYES!"

While the leader screamed and clawed at his face, Shen He scrambled to his feet and ran. He didn't look back, didn't slow down, didn't stop until his lungs burned and his legs threatened to give out.

The path ended at a crossroads. To the left, the road wound toward a small village that clung to the mountain's base. To the right, it led deeper into the forest, toward the untamed wilds where demon beasts roamed and cultivators feared to tread.

Shen He chose the forest.

The trees closed around him like a cage, their branches weaving together to block out the fading sunlight. Strange sounds echoed through the underbrush—the rustle of leaves, the snap of twigs, the distant roar of something large and hungry.

But none of that mattered. What mattered was the paper in his hand, and the knowledge burning in his chest.

The sect master had said the Meridian Restoration Pill didn't work. That it killed everyone who tried to use it.

But Shen He had read the original scrolls. He'd seen the diagrams, the calculations, the meticulous notes of the ancient alchemists who'd created it. And he'd noticed something the sect master had conveniently omitted.

The pill hadn't failed because the formula was flawed. It had failed because the alchemists had been missing one crucial ingredient.

The third ingredient.

And Shen He knew what it was.

He stopped walking, his heart pounding against his ribs. The realization had hit him during the trial, when Lin Xue had looked at him with such cold, unwavering certainty. She knew he'd stolen the formula. She knew he'd attempted the refinement. But she didn't know what he'd discovered.

The third ingredient wasn't a plant. It wasn't a mineral. It wasn't anything that could be harvested or bought or stolen.

It was blood. The blood of a Meridian Beast.

The ancient texts had mentioned them only in passing—creatures born with twisted, corrupted qi channels that allowed them to absorb and manipulate energy in ways that defied natural law. Their blood, if properly refined, could stabilize the volatile energies of the Meridian Restoration Pill, turning it from a death sentence into a rebirth.

But Meridian Beasts had been hunted to extinction a thousand years ago. Or so the records claimed.

Shen He sank to his knees, the paper crumpling in his grip. Even if he knew what he needed, how could he possibly obtain it? He was a cripple with no cultivation, no resources, no allies. The closest Meridian Beast was likely a corpse buried in some ancient battlefield, its blood long since dried and worthless.

"Damn it," he whispered, his voice cracking. "Damn it all."

He sat there for a long time, the forest growing darker around him. A part of him wanted to give up, to lie down and let the beasts have him. What was the point of fighting? He'd been born broken, had spent his entire life being told he was worthless, and now he'd been cast out with nothing but a useless formula and a dying dream.

But then he thought of Elder Wu's smug face. Of Lin Xue's cold eyes. Of the sect master's dismissive tone when he'd said the pill didn't work.

They'd called him a cripple. A failure. A nobody who would never amount to anything.

*I'll show them,* he thought, the anger rising like a tide. *I'll show them all.*

A twig snapped behind him.

Shen He whirled around, his heart leaping into his throat. A figure stood at the edge of the clearing, silhouetted against the last rays of sunlight. Tall. Lean. Wearing robes of deep crimson that seemed to drink the light.

"Who's there?" Shen He demanded, his voice steadier than he felt.

The figure stepped forward, and the light fell across a face that was both beautiful and terrifying. A woman, perhaps thirty years old, with sharp features and eyes that glowed faintly red in the twilight. Her hair was the color of dried blood, pulled back in a severe ponytail that fell to her waist.

"You're the one," she said, her voice like velvet wrapped around steel. "The alchemist who tried to recreate the Meridian Restoration Pill."

Shen He's blood ran cold. "How do you know about that?"

The woman smiled, and it was not a kind smile. "I've been watching you, Shen He. For weeks, ever since you first accessed the restricted section. I was curious to see if you would succeed where so many others had failed."

"You're from the sect?"

"Hardly." She laughed, a sound like breaking glass. "I'm from somewhere far more interesting. A place where cripples like you are given the tools to become something more."

Shen He's mind raced. This woman knew too much. She'd been watching him, which meant she had access to the sect's grounds without being detected. That alone made her dangerous.

"What do you want?"

"Your formula," she said simply. "And your services. The Alchemy Guild has need of someone with your... unique talents."

"The Alchemy Guild?" Shen He had heard of them, of course. Everyone had. They were the shadowy organization that controlled the trade of pills and elixirs across the continent. They were also rumored to be involved in far darker pursuits—experiments that blurred the line between alchemy and forbidden sorcery.

"Don't look so surprised," the woman said. "You think the Azure Cloud Sect is the only power that takes an interest in promising alchemists? Your talent is wasted on them. They see you as a cripple. We see you as an opportunity."

"And if I refuse?"

The woman's smile widened, and her eyes flared brighter. "Then you'll die here, alone and forgotten, just as everyone always expected. But that's not what you want, is it? You want to prove them wrong. You want to show them what you're capable of."

She extended her hand. In her palm rested a single pill, black as obsidian and pulsing with a faint, malevolent light.

"This is a gift," she said. "A taste of what the Guild can offer. Swallow it, and your crippled meridians will open. You'll be able to cultivate. You'll be able to fight. You'll be able to take your revenge on everyone who ever wronged you."

Shen He stared at the pill. It was wrong. Everything about it screamed danger, from its color to the way it seemed to writhe in the woman's palm. But the promise it held was irresistible.

"You'll be mine, of course," the woman continued. "Bound to the Guild through a contract that cannot be broken. But isn't that a small price to pay for power?"

Shen He's hand trembled as he reached out. His fingers brushed against the pill's surface, and a jolt of energy shot through his arm, making his teeth ache.

*This is insane,* he thought. *I don't even know who she is. I don't know what the Guild wants from me. This could be a trap.*

But the alternative was death. A slow, cold death in the forest, alone and forgotten.

He closed his eyes.

And swallowed the pill.

The effect was immediate. Fire erupted in his chest, spreading through his veins like molten metal. His meridians—those twisted, broken channels that had never carried qi—suddenly blazed with light. He screamed, his body convulsing as the pill rewrote the very fabric of his existence.
