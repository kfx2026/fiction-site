Chapter 8: The Corpse That Spoke

~1.5k words · Updated 2026-06-22

The mountain path twisted downward through layers of mist, each step carrying Shen He further from the only home he'd ever known. His boots crunched against loose gravel, and somewhere in the distance, a crow cawed three times before falling silent.

*Bad omen*, he thought. *But then again, what isn't these days?*

He'd walked for two hours when the forest began to change. The bamboo gave way to gnarled oaks with twisted roots that seemed to reach for his ankles. The air grew thick, heavy with the smell of damp earth and something else—something metallic.

Blood.

Shen He stopped, his hand instinctively going to his waist. But he had no weapon. He'd never needed one. Pills weren't much use in a fight.

The road ahead curved around a massive boulder, and from the other side came a sound that froze the blood in his veins.

A wet, gurgling cough.

Then, a voice. Weak. Desperate.

"Please... help..."

Shen He's mind screamed at him to run. He was banished, powerless, a cripple alone in the wilderness. Whatever waited around that corner could kill him without effort.

But the voice sounded human. And it was dying.

He moved forward, keeping close to the trees, his heart pounding so hard he could feel it in his throat. When he rounded the boulder, what he saw made him stop dead.

A man lay sprawled across the path, his robes torn and soaked through with blood. The fabric was rich—deep purple silk embroidered with silver threads that formed the character for "Medicine" across his chest. An alchemist. A high-ranking one, by the look of his robes.

But it was his face that drew Shen He's attention. Young, maybe twenty-five, with sharp features and skin so pale it was almost blue. His eyes were open, staring at the sky with a glassy emptiness that spoke of approaching death.

"Help me," the man whispered, his lips barely moving. "They... they took it..."

Shen He dropped to his knees beside him, his hands moving automatically to assess the wounds. Three deep gashes across the man's chest, each one precise, surgical. A fourth wound in his side, where something sharp had been driven in and twisted.

"This is bad," Shen He muttered. "I need—"

"Pills," the man gasped. "Belt pouch... left side..."

Shen He found the pouch and opened it. Inside were five pills, each one glowing with a soft golden light. He recognized them instantly. Grade-3 Life Preservation Pills. Each one worth more spirit stones than he'd seen in his entire life.

"Take one," the man said. "Crush it... mix with water... apply to wounds..."

"You're losing too much blood. You need to ingest it."

"No!" The man's hand shot out and grabbed Shen He's wrist with surprising strength. "Topical only. The pill... it's too strong for me. My meridians... they're shattered. If I absorb the qi directly, my heart will stop."

Shen He froze. That shouldn't be possible. Life Preservation Pills were designed to be gentle, to flood the body with healing energy without overwhelming the recipient. Unless—

*Unless the attacker already shattered his meridians completely.*

Which meant the attacker knew exactly what they were doing. This wasn't a robbery gone wrong. This was an assassination.

"Who did this to you?" Shen He asked as he crushed the pill between two stones and mixed the golden powder with water from his canteen.

"Doesn't matter," the man said, his voice growing weaker. "They got what they wanted. The formula... it's gone."

"What formula?"

The man's eyes focused on Shen He for the first time, and something flickered in them. Recognition. Or perhaps desperation.

"You're from the Azure Cloud Sect," he said. "I can tell by your robes. Outer disciple."

"Former outer disciple. I was exiled an hour ago."

A wet laugh escaped the man's lips. "Exiled. Good. That's... that's good. Means you have nothing to lose."

He coughed, and blood sprayed from his lips. Shen He pressed the mixture into the wounds, and the man hissed in pain but didn't pull away.

"Listen to me," the man said, his voice urgent now. "I don't have much time. My name is Wei Chen. I'm a Grade-5 Alchemist from the Celestial Pill Pavilion."

Shen He's hands stopped moving. The Celestial Pill Pavilion was one of the three great alchemy organizations in the empire. They produced pills for kings and sect masters. Their alchemists were treated like royalty.

"Someone from your own sect did this?"

"No. Someone from *inside* your sect. The Azure Cloud Sect."

"That's impossible. We don't have alchemists skilled enough to—"

"They didn't hire an alchemist. They hired me. Three months ago, a man approached me with a proposition. He offered me ten thousand spirit stones to create a single pill. A pill that could restore shattered meridians."

Shen He's blood ran cold. "The Meridian Restoration Pill."

Wei Chen's eyes widened. "You know about it?"

"I tried to make one. It... didn't go well."

"Then you know why it's forbidden. The formula was sealed away because it's incomplete. The ancient alchemists who designed it never finished the final step. Everyone who's tried to complete it has failed. The pill kills the user."

"That's what the sect master told me."

"The sect master is a liar."

Shen He's hands stopped moving. "What?"

"The pill doesn't kill the user. It *saves* them." Wei Chen grabbed Shen He's arm, his grip weak but desperate. "I completed the formula. I found the missing ingredient. The pill works. It *works*."

"Then why—"

"Because the sect master doesn't want it to work. He wants the formula destroyed. He's been hunting down everyone who's ever tried to replicate it. I'm the seventh alchemist he's had killed in the past year."

Shen He's mind reeled. The sect master. The man who had raised him, who had given him a place when no one else would. The man who had just exiled him with cold indifference.

Was it possible?

"Why?" Shen He asked. "Why would he want to suppress a pill that could heal crippled cultivators?"

"Because crippled cultivators are useful," Wei Chen said, his voice dropping to a whisper. "They're easy to control. They can be used as pawns, as test subjects, as sacrifices. A cripple who can suddenly cultivate becomes a threat. And the sect master doesn't like threats."

Wei Chen's hand went to his chest, fumbling beneath his robes. He pulled out a small jade slip, no bigger than his thumb. It was cracked, one corner chipped off, but it still glowed with a faint inner light.

"The formula," he said, pressing it into Shen He's hand. "The complete formula. Take it. Finish what I started."

"I can't," Shen He said. "I'm a cripple. I have no cultivation. I can't even refine a proper pill."

"You already tried. You got close. The explosion in the outer compound—that proves you have potential." Wei Chen's eyes were growing dim, his voice fading. "The missing ingredient... it's not a herb or a mineral. It's a technique. A way to weave the qi through the pill in a specific pattern. The ancient alchemists couldn't figure it out because they were too focused on ingredients. But it's not about ingredients. It's about control."

"Control of what?"

"Life force. Your own life force. The pill requires... a sacrifice. A piece of the alchemist's own vitality to complete the transformation." Wei Chen's grip tightened. "I tried it. On myself. That's why my meridians are shattered. It worked, but I didn't have enough strength to complete the process. I need... I need someone else to try."

"You want me to test a pill that shattered your meridians?"

"I want you to *complete* it. The theory is sound. The execution was flawed. You're different. You have no cultivation, which means your life force is pure. Uncorrupted by external qi. That's what the pill needs."

Shen He looked at the jade slip in his hand. It felt warm, almost alive. Inside it was the answer to everything he'd ever wanted. The ability to cultivate. The ability to be more than a cripple.

"I don't know if I can trust you," Shen He said.

"Good," Wei Chen whispered. "Trust no one. Not the sect master. Not the elders. Not even yourself." His eyes closed. "But trust the formula. It's the only thing that won't betray you."

His hand went limp. His chest stopped moving.

Wei Chen was dead.

Shen He sat there for a long moment, holding the jade slip, feeling the weight of it in his palm. Then he heard something that made every hair on his body stand on end.

Footsteps. Multiple sets. Coming down the mountain path.

He looked up just in time to see figures emerging from the mist. Six of them. All wearing the robes of Azure Cloud Sect inner disciples. And at their head, walking with the casual grace of a predator who had already cornered its prey—

Lin Xue.

Her eyes found him immediately, and her lips curled into a smile that didn't reach her eyes.

"Shen He," she said, her voice carrying through the mist like a bell. "The sect master sends his regards."

In his hand, the jade slip pulsed once, warm and urgent.

*Run*, it seemed to say. *Before it's too late.*

Shen He ran.
