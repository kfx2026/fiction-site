CHAPTER 9: The Weight of Whispers

The night air carried the stench of fish and brine from the nearby docks. Zhao Feng sat cross-legged on a crate of low-grade qi stones, the lantern casting long shadows across his ledger. The numbers danced before his eyes—figures that told a story of modest beginnings, but not yet of empire.

A knock came at the warehouse door. Three sharp raps, then two soft ones. The signal.

Zhao Feng rose, his muscles tense. He opened the door to find Xiao Chen slipping through the gap like a shadow, his thin frame barely visible in the darkness. The boy's eyes were wide, his breath coming in short gasps.

"He's not alone," Xiao Chen whispered.

Zhao Feng's hand drifted to the dagger hidden beneath his robes. "Wu Chen?"

"No. Someone else. A cultivator." Xiao Chen's voice trembled. "I followed Wu Chen from the Jade Pavilion. He met with a man wearing blue silk—the kind with silver thread woven through it. They talked for an hour in a private room."

Blue silk with silver thread. The uniform of the Cloud Serpent Sect's outer disciples.

Zhao Feng's mind raced. The Cloud Serpent Sect was one of the minor sects in the region, but even a minor sect cultivator could crush a mortal merchant like an ant. If Wu Chen had aligned himself with them, this was worse than Zhao Feng had anticipated.

"Did you hear what they said?"

Xiao Chen nodded, his face pale. "They know about your qi stones. The cultivator said you were 'an interesting variable' that needed to be 'removed before the tournament.' Wu Chen agreed. He said he'd handle it tonight."

Tonight.

Zhao Feng looked around the warehouse. His stockpile. His future. Everything he had built in the past two months was sitting in these crates. And now, a Cloud Serpent cultivator wanted him dead.

"How long do we have?"

"Wu Chen was gathering men when I left. Maybe an hour. Maybe less."

An hour. Not enough time to move everything. Not enough time to find help. Not enough time to do anything except make a choice.

Zhao Feng's mind flashed to his past life. He'd faced worse odds. He'd lost everything and rebuilt it a dozen times. But back then, he'd had connections, money, power. Now he had a leaking warehouse, a street rat, and a pile of rocks.

"Help me move the ledger and the high-grade stones," he said, his voice calm. "Everything else stays."

Xiao Chen stared at him. "But—"

"If we try to move everything, we'll be caught. Better to save what matters and live to fight another day." Zhao Feng grabbed a bag and began stuffing it with the highest quality qi stones. "There's a safe house two streets over. An old apothecary shop that's been closed for years. I bought it last week."

"You planned for this?"

"I plan for everything."

They worked in silence, the minutes ticking by like drops of blood from a wound. Xiao Chen moved with surprising speed, his small hands deftly packing the spirit grass and the most valuable stones. Within twenty minutes, they had two bags filled with the core of Zhao Feng's wealth.

Then the door splintered.

Wood exploded inward as a figure in blue silk stepped through the gap. He was young, maybe twenty, with a sharp face and eyes that held the cold arrogance of someone who had never known failure. In his hand, a sword glowed with pale blue light.

"Zhao Feng," the cultivator said, his voice smooth as oil. "I was hoping to catch you here."

Zhao Feng straightened, placing himself between the cultivator and Xiao Chen. "And you are?"

"Senior Disciple Liu of the Cloud Serpent Sect." The man smiled, but there was no warmth in it. "I've heard interesting things about you, merchant. A nobody who suddenly appears with a fortune in qi stones. A mortal who deals in cultivation resources as if he has a thousand years of experience."

"I'm flattered."

"Don't be." Liu stepped forward, his sword still glowing. "I'm here to offer you a choice. Give me everything you have, and I'll let you live. Refuse, and I'll take it anyway, then burn this place to the ground with you inside."

Zhao Feng's hand tightened on the dagger. He was no match for a cultivator. Even a low-level one like Liu could kill him with a flick of his wrist. But Zhao Feng had something Liu didn't have: forty years of understanding how the cultivation world truly worked.

"Before I decide," Zhao Feng said, his voice steady, "I have a question. Who told you about me? Was it Wu Chen? Or someone higher up?"

Liu's smile widened. "Why does it matter?"

"Because if Wu Chen was the one who brought you here, then you're working for a desperate man. And desperate men make mistakes." Zhao Feng reached into his robe and pulled out a small jade slip, holding it up. "Did Wu Chen tell you about this?"

Liu's eyes narrowed. "What is that?"

"A record. Every transaction I've made since I started. Every person I've dealt with. And a list of names—including yours, Senior Disciple Liu, and the exact amount you owe to a certain gambling den in the eastern district."

The cultivator's face went pale.

"You see," Zhao Feng continued, "I make it a point to know my enemies before they know me. You owe fifty thousand spirit coins to the Golden Lotus Gambling House. Your sect doesn't know. Your master doesn't know. But I do."

Liu's sword trembled. "That's a lie."

"Is it? Then why are you shaking?" Zhao Feng took a step forward, his voice dropping. "I'm offering you a different deal, Senior Disciple. Walk away. Tell Wu Chen you couldn't find me. And in return, I'll forget I ever saw your name on that list."

For a long moment, the only sound was the creaking of the warehouse walls and the distant cry of gulls. Liu's face cycled through rage, fear, and calculation. His sword flickered, the light dimming.

"You're bluffing," he finally said.

"I'm really not." Zhao Feng held up the jade slip. "This is going to a very curious sect elder if anything happens to me tonight. You know how the Cloud Serpent Sect feels about gambling debts. They'd expel you. Maybe worse."

Liu's jaw clenched. He looked at the bags of qi stones, then at Zhao Feng, then back at the bags. The greed in his eyes warred with the fear.

"Fine," he spat. "You live tonight. But if that information ever surfaces—"

"It won't, as long as you keep your end of the bargain." Zhao Feng stepped aside, gesturing toward the broken door. "Now, if you'll excuse me, I have business to attend to."

Liu glared at him, then turned and stalked out into the night. His footsteps faded into the darkness, leaving only the sound of Xiao Chen's ragged breathing.

The boy let out a shaky exhale. "That was... close."

"Too close." Zhao Feng grabbed the bags. "We need to move. Now."

They slipped out the back entrance, into the maze of alleys that wound through the merchant district. The moon was hidden behind clouds, casting the streets into deep shadow. Zhao Feng led the way, his feet remembering paths he'd walked in another life.

The old apothecary shop stood at the end of a dead-end alley, its windows boarded up and its sign hanging crooked. Zhao Feng produced a key and unlocked the door, ushering Xiao Chen inside.

The interior smelled of dust and dried herbs. Shelves lined the walls, empty except for a few cracked jars. But in the back room, hidden behind a false wall, was a small chamber with a bed, a table, and a locking chest.

Zhao Feng set down the bags and lit a candle. "We'll stay here until things cool down. Then I need to find out who else is pulling Wu Chen's strings."

Xiao Chen sat on the bed, his legs dangling over the edge. "You think there's someone else?"

"I know there is." Zhao Feng opened the chest and began transferring the qi stones inside. "Wu Chen doesn't have the authority to hire a Cloud Serpent cultivator. Someone with real power is backing him. Someone who wants me dead before the tournament."

"Who?"

Zhao Feng paused, a single qi stone in his hand. In his past life, there had been a man who'd tried to destroy him during the Immortal Ascension Tournament. A man from the Zhao family—his own nephew, Zhao Lei, who'd seen him as a threat to the family's power.

But that was in another life. In this one, Zhao Lei was still a child, barely ten years old. It couldn't be him.

"I don't know yet," he admitted. "But I will."

A knock came from the front of the shop.

Both of them froze. Zhao Feng's hand went to his dagger again. Xiao Chen slid off the bed, his eyes wide.

"Stay here," Zhao Feng whispered. "Don't make a sound."

He crept through the dark shop, his heart pounding. The knock came again—three sharp raps, then two soft ones. The signal.

But only two people knew that signal. Him and Xiao Chen.

He reached the door and peered through a crack in the boards. A figure stood in the alley, wrapped in a dark cloak. The face was hidden, but the posture was familiar. Confident. Waiting.

Zhao Feng unlocked the door and opened it a crack.

"Who are you?"

The figure lowered the hood, revealing a woman's face—sharp features, dark eyes, and a scar that ran from her temple to her jaw. She was young, maybe twenty-five, but her eyes held the weight of someone much older.

"Someone who wants to make you an offer," she said, her voice low and rough. "I know what you're planning, Zhao Feng. The tournament. The hidden techniques. The girl who can control lightning."

Zhao Feng's blood ran cold. "I don't know what you're talking about."

"You will." She reached into her cloak and pulled out a jade slip, identical to the one he'd shown Liu. "I have information you need. In exchange, I want your help. There's a sect that needs to fall, and you're the only one who can make it happen."

"Which sect?"

Her smile was cold. "The Cloud Serpent Sect."

The candle behind him flickered and died, plunging them both into darkness. When Zhao Feng's eyes adjusted, the woman was gone, leaving only the jade slip lying on the ground where she'd stood.

He picked it up, his hands trembling. Inside the slip, he could sense a single message, pressed into the jade with spiritual energy:

*"They killed my family. Help me destroy them, and I'll give you the tournament."*

Beneath the words, a name: *Lin Xue.*

Zhao Feng's breath caught. Lin Xue. The girl who'd won the Immortal Ascension Tournament. The nobody who'd shocked the world with her lightning techniques.

She was standing in his alley, asking for his help.

And she knew exactly who he was.
