CHAPTER 10: The Witness Takes the Stand

The courtroom fell silent. Every eye turned to Elara, waiting for her response. She could feel the weight of their expectations, their suspicion, their hunger for her downfall.

*Think, Elara. Think.*

The Divine Witness of Truth. A being that could see through every lie, every deception, every carefully constructed facade. If she agreed, her secret would be exposed within seconds. The real Demon Empress was dead—had been dead for three months now, her soul obliterated by the very poison meant to kill Elara's original body. And Elara Chen, corporate lawyer from another world, had been wearing her face ever since.

But if she refused...

"The defendant hesitates," the prosecutor said, his voice dripping with false concern. "Surely the former Demon Empress has nothing to hide?"

"I'm considering the logistics," Elara replied, her voice steady despite the panic clawing at her chest. "The Divine Witness is not a being to be summoned lightly. It requires specific preparations, offerings, and a binding circle that takes at least three days to construct."

She was buying time. She had no idea if any of that was true, but she'd read enough fantasy novels in her previous life to know that summoning powerful beings usually required complicated rituals.

The hero—she really needed to learn his actual name, she thought—raised an eyebrow. "And how would you know that?"

"I was the Demon Empress for three centuries," Elara said, letting a hint of her predecessor's cold arrogance seep into her voice. "I've studied more esoteric texts than your entire sect has members."

A murmur ran through the crowd. The judges exchanged glances.

"She's not wrong," the Arbiter's voice echoed. "The summoning of the Divine Witness requires a three-day preparation period. It's in the celestial code of conduct."

Elara almost sagged with relief. Three days. She had three days to figure out how to survive a being that could see through every lie she'd ever told.

Three days to find a loophole.

*Three days to either escape or die.*

"The court acknowledges the delay," the head judge said. "The Divine Witness will be summoned in three days' time. Until then, the defendant will be held in the Celestial Prison."

"Under whose jurisdiction?" Elara asked quickly. "If I'm to be tried by celestial law, then I should be held by celestial authorities, not the Celestial Sword Sect."

The hero's eyes narrowed. "That's unprecedented—"

"It's standard procedure," Elara interrupted. "Article 47 of the Inter-Realm Extradition Treaty. A defendant awaiting trial cannot be held by the accusing party. It creates a conflict of interest."

She'd made that up completely. But she was betting that no one in this world had actually read the treaty's fine print.

The head judge's lips twitched. "The defendant is correct. She will be held in the Celestial Prison, under the watch of the Arbiter's office."

The hero slammed his fist on the table. "This is absurd! She's manipulating the system—"

"She's using the law," the Arbiter corrected. "Which is exactly what this trial is supposed to be about. Unless you're afraid of a fair proceeding?"

The hero's face reddened, but he said nothing. The judges banged their gavels, and the session was adjourned.

---

The Celestial Prison was nothing like what Elara had expected.

Instead of dark cells and iron bars, she found herself in a floating island surrounded by waterfalls of starlight. The air was crisp and clean, and the only indication that she was a prisoner was the golden chain that bound her ankle to the island's core.

"Comfortable?" the Arbiter asked, appearing beside her.

"Surprisingly, yes." Elara sat down on a patch of grass that felt more luxurious than any five-star hotel bed. "I expected worse."

"The Celestial Prison is designed for rehabilitation, not punishment." The Arbiter sat across from her, her white robes pooling around her like melted snow. "At least for those who haven't been convicted yet."

"And if I'm convicted?"

"You'll be moved to the Abyss. That's less comfortable."

Elara nodded slowly. "I need to know something. The Divine Witness—can it be fooled?"

The Arbiter's expression didn't change, but there was a shift in her energy, a subtle tension. "No. It is absolute truth. It sees through all deceptions, all illusions, all lies. Even self-deception."

"Even memories?"

"Especially memories." The Arbiter leaned forward. "Why do you ask?"

Elara met her gaze. She had a choice to make. Trust this woman, or try to find another way out.

*When in doubt, make an ally.*

"Because I'm not the Demon Empress," Elara said quietly.

The Arbiter didn't react. She simply nodded, as if she'd known all along. "I suspected as much. The real Demon Empress would never have requested a jury. She would have burned the courtroom down instead."

"She's dead. I woke up in her body three months ago, moments before her execution."

"Ah." The Arbiter's eyes softened. "That explains the change in behavior. I'd heard rumors—the Demon Empress suddenly showing mercy, using legal tactics instead of brute force. The cultivation world was confused."

"They thought I was planning something."

"Most did, yes." The Arbiter paused. "But not everyone. Some of us wondered if something else had happened."

"Are you going to report me?"

"To whom? The Divine Witness will reveal everything in three days anyway." The Arbiter smiled, and for a moment, she looked almost human. "Besides, I've always believed that the law should serve justice, not convenience. If you truly are not the Demon Empress, then you shouldn't be punished for her crimes."

"That's... surprisingly progressive."

"I've been alive for six thousand years. I've seen enough injustice to know that the system isn't always right." The Arbiter stood. "I'll do what I can to help you. But I can't stop the Divine Witness. No one can."

"Then I need to find a way to survive the truth."

The Arbiter tilted her head. "There is one possibility. A legal technicality, but it might work."

"Tell me."

"The Divine Witness compels truth, but it doesn't compel *complete* truth. If you can frame your answers in a way that is technically true but misleading, the Witness cannot penalize you. It's a loophole that's been used before."

Elara's mind raced. "So I need to speak in half-truths."

"Carefully constructed half-truths, yes. But it's dangerous. If the Witness detects that you're intentionally deceiving, the penalty is severe."

"What's the penalty?"

"Immediate truth exposure. Every thought, every memory, every intention laid bare for all to see." The Arbiter's voice dropped. "And then the trial proceeds with the full knowledge of who you really are."

"Wonderful."

"I'll leave you to prepare." The Arbiter began to fade. "Oh, and Elara? The hero's name is Yang Chen. You should know who you're fighting."

And then she was gone.

---

Elara spent the next three days doing two things: sleeping and thinking.

She'd learned in her previous life that exhaustion was the enemy of good strategy. A tired brain made mistakes. So she rested, ate the strange but delicious fruit that appeared on the island, and let her subconscious work on the problem.

When she wasn't resting, she was practicing.

She'd discovered that she could still access some of the Demon Empress's memories, even three months after taking over her body. They were fragmented, incomplete, but useful. She practiced recalling specific events, then reframing them in ways that were technically true but misleading.

*The Demon Empress killed the Elder of the Heavenly Flame Sect.*

True. But she'd killed him in self-defense, after he'd tried to assassinate her first. Elara practiced saying "I killed the Elder" without adding the context. It wasn't a lie. But it wasn't the whole truth either.

*The Demon Empress stole the Celestial Pearl.*

Also true. But she'd stolen it to prevent the Celestial Sword Sect from using it to start a war. Elara practiced saying "I took the Pearl" and letting people assume the worst.

By the third day, she'd crafted a web of half-truths that could, theoretically, get her through the Divine Witness's interrogation without revealing her true identity.

*Theoretically.*

---

The Divine Witness was beautiful.

That was Elara's first thought when she was led back into the courtroom. Floating above the center of the room was a being made entirely of light, shifting through colors that had no names in any human language. It had no face, no form, no features—and yet Elara felt like it was looking directly into her soul.

"The Divine Witness has been summoned," the head judge announced. "Defendant, please step forward."

Elara walked to the center of the room, her heart pounding but her face calm. She'd spent three days preparing for this moment. She was ready.

*I am Elara Chen, corporate lawyer. I am not the Demon Empress. But they don't need to know that.*

The Divine Witness pulsed, and a voice resonated through the courtroom—not through sound, but directly into everyone's minds.

**I AM HERE. I WILL SPEAK THE TRUTH. I WILL HEAR THE TRUTH. WHO SHALL BE QUESTIONED FIRST?**

"The defendant," the prosecutor said immediately.

**VERY WELL.**

The light shifted, focusing on Elara. She felt a pressure in her mind, like someone was gently but insistently opening a door.

**TELL ME YOUR NAME.**

"Elara Chen," she said.

**THAT IS YOUR TRUE NAME. BUT YOU WEAR THE BODY OF THE DEMON EMPRESS. HOW DID YOU COME TO BE HERE?**

"I was placed here by forces beyond my control," Elara said carefully. "I woke up in this body three months ago, with no memory of how I arrived."

That was technically true. She had no memory of how she'd arrived—because she'd been dead, and then she'd woken up. The "forces beyond her control" were vague enough to cover any interpretation.

**DID YOU KILL THE DEMON EMPRESS?**

"No."

She hadn't. The Demon Empress had been poisoned by her own servants, acting on orders from the Celestial Sword Sect. Elara had just been the unlucky soul who'd inherited the body.

**DID YOU COMMIT THE CRIMES FOR WHICH THE DEMON EMPRESS WAS CHARGED?**

"No."

She hadn't. The Demon Empress had committed those crimes, but Elara Chen had never raised a hand against anyone in this world.

**ARE YOU THE DEMON EMPRESS?**

Elara paused. This was the critical question. If she said no, the Divine Witness would know she was telling the truth—but then the prosecution would argue that she was an imposter, which could lead to her being executed for impersonation. If she said yes...

"No," she said. "I am Elara Chen. I am not the Demon Empress."

The courtroom erupted.

But then the Divine Witness spoke again, and everyone fell silent.

**SHE SPEAKS THE TRUTH. THE BEING BEFORE ME IS NOT THE DEMON EMPRESS. THE DEMON EMPRESS'S SOUL IS EXTINCT.**

The hero—Yang Chen—stood up, his face pale. "Then who is she? And why does she have the Demon Empress's body?"
