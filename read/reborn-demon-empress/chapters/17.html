CHAPTER 17: The Witness in the Mirror

The courtroom held its breath. Elara's mind, usually a fortress of logic and strategy, had gone completely silent. The Divine Witness of Truth. A being that could see through every lie, every half-truth, every carefully constructed facade.

*I'm dead.*

"Acceptable," the head judge said before Elara could formulate a response. "The Divine Witness is standard procedure for arbitration cases involving identity disputes."

"I didn't agree to—"

"Your acceptance is implied by your request for a jury," the judge continued, cutting her off with a wave of his hand. "The Celestial Code is quite clear on this matter. You cannot claim the protections of our laws while hiding behind a false identity."

Elara's blood turned to ice. *They already know. Or at least, they suspect.*

She glanced at the hero. He was watching her with that calculating look, the one that suggested he'd been playing a much longer game than she'd realized. He hadn't just accused her of crimes—he'd challenged the very foundation of her existence.

"The Witness will arrive shortly," the Arbiter's voice echoed. "I suggest you prepare yourself, Elara Chen."

*Prepare myself.* For what? To have every secret ripped from her chest and displayed for the entire cultivation world to see? To have them learn that the fearsome Demon Empress was actually a corporate lawyer from another dimension who couldn't even light a candle with qi?

*Or worse—to have them learn that I'm not the Demon Empress at all.*

She'd spent the past month building alliances, planting seeds of doubt, carefully constructing a reputation as someone who couldn't be underestimated. If they discovered the truth, all of that would crumble. And then what? Annihilation. Complete erasure.

*No.* Elara's jaw tightened. *I didn't survive my first death to roll over for this.*

She raised her head, meeting the hero's gaze directly. "Fine. Call your Witness. But I have conditions of my own."

The hero's eyebrows rose. "You're in no position to make demands."

"I'm in the perfect position." Elara turned to face the judges, her voice steady despite the terror racing through her veins. "Under Article 17 of the Celestial Code, a defendant who submits to the Divine Witness is entitled to reciprocal revelation. If the Witness examines me, it must also examine my accuser and every witness brought against me."

The head judge's ancient face showed a flicker of surprise. "That is... technically correct."

"I want the hero—" she paused, realizing she didn't even know his real name, "—whatever his name is, to submit to the Witness as well. And every member of the Celestial Sword Sect who testifies against me."

"Impossible," the hero said quickly. "I am not on trial here."

"Then I withdraw my consent." Elara crossed her arms. "If you want the truth from me, you get the truth from everyone. That's how fair trials work."

The Arbiter's voice came from above, carrying a hint of amusement. "She has a point. The code is clear."

The judges conferred in whispers that Elara couldn't hear. She used the time to study the room, cataloging faces, noting who seemed sympathetic and who looked eager for her blood. A young woman in the back row was watching her with something that might have been admiration. An old man with a gnarled staff was scowling openly.

*Good. I need to know my enemies.*

"Very well," the head judge announced. "The defendant's request is granted. All parties who testify will be subject to the Divine Witness's scrutiny."

The hero's face tightened. He hadn't expected this. Good.

"However," the judge continued, "there is a complication. The Divine Witness manifests differently for each individual. What it reveals, and how it reveals it, is beyond our control. You may not like what comes to light, defendant."

"I never expected to."

A ripple of energy passed through the courtroom. The air grew heavy, charged with something ancient and terrible. The cloud walls began to darken, turning gray, then black. The starfield ceiling went out, plunging the room into absolute darkness.

Then, a single point of light appeared at the center of the room.

It grew slowly, expanding like a flower opening its petals. But this was no flower—it was an eye. A massive, unblinking eye that hovered above the floor, its iris swirling with colors that didn't exist in nature.

"The Divine Witness," someone whispered.

Elara felt its gaze upon her, and for a moment, she forgot how to breathe. It wasn't just looking at her—it was *reading* her. Every memory, every thought, every hidden corner of her soul was laid bare.

*I'm sorry, Mom. I'm sorry I couldn't visit more. I'm sorry I died before I could tell you—*

The Witness's eye turned, and the pressure on her mind lessened. It was moving to someone else. The hero.

He stood frozen, his face pale. For the first time since she'd met him, he looked genuinely afraid.

"Begin," the head judge commanded.

The first witness was an elder from the Celestial Sword Sect, a white-bearded man with eyes like chips of ice. He stepped forward, and the Witness's eye fixed upon him.

"State your name and relationship to the defendant," the Arbiter said.

"I am Elder Feng of the Celestial Sword Sect. I have known the Demon Empress for three hundred years." His voice was steady, but Elara noticed a bead of sweat on his temple. "She is a monster. She slaughtered my disciples. She—"

"Stop." The Arbiter's voice was sharp. "The Witness has detected a falsehood."

Elder Feng's face went white. "I—I swear—"

"Your swearing means nothing." The Arbiter floated down from above, materializing as a woman made of smoke and starlight. "The Witness has detected that you are omitting key context. You claim she slaughtered your disciples, but you neglect to mention that they attacked her first. In her own palace. Without provocation."

The courtroom erupted.

"She's lying!" Elder Feng shouted. "The Demon Empress is manipulating—"

"The Witness does not lie." The Arbiter turned to Elara. "Continue your questioning, defendant."

Elara's mind raced. *I can work with this.* "Thank you, Arbiter. Elder Feng, did your disciples have orders to assassinate me?"

"No! They were merely—"

"Careful," Elara interrupted. "The Witness is watching."

Elder Feng's mouth snapped shut. His face twisted with fury, but he couldn't speak. The truth was strangling him.

"Let me rephrase." Elara stepped closer, her heels clicking on the obsidian floor. "Did you give them orders to assassinate me?"

Silence.

"The Witness requires an answer," the Arbiter said.

"...Yes." The word came out like poison.

"And did you also order them to destroy my cultivation artifacts? The ones that were legally registered under my ownership?"

Another long pause. "Yes."

"And did you falsify reports to the Celestial Council claiming that I was plotting war against the mortal realm?"

Elder Feng's hands were shaking. "The Demon Empress is a threat to—"

"That's not what I asked."

"Yes," he hissed. "I falsified the reports."

The courtroom was dead silent. Elara could feel the weight of a hundred gazes upon her, but she didn't look away from Elder Feng.

"One more question," she said softly. "Did you know that I had no cultivation when you sent your disciples to kill me?"

Elder Feng's eyes went wide. "What? That's impossible. You're the Demon Empress—"

"The Witness," the Arbiter interrupted, "confirms that the defendant speaks the truth. She currently possesses no cultivation."

Gasps. Whispers. The hero's face had gone pale.

*Well,* Elara thought, *there goes my secret.*

But she'd known this was coming. Better to control the revelation than to have it forced out of her.

"Yes," she said, turning to face the judges. "I am the Demon Empress, but I have no cultivation. I was poisoned, betrayed, and left for dead. The Celestial Sword Sect—" she pointed at Elder Feng, "—took advantage of my weakness to destroy me. They didn't want justice. They wanted to eliminate a rival without consequences."

"You can't prove—" Elder Feng started.

"I just did." Elara smiled, but there was no warmth in it. "The Witness confirmed my truth and your lies. Now, if you'll excuse me, I believe it's my accuser's turn to testify."

The hero stepped forward, his jaw tight. "This changes nothing. Even if the Celestial Sword Sect acted improperly, that doesn't absolve you of your crimes against the cultivation world."

"Then prove them," Elara said. "Under the Witness."

The hero's eyes met hers, and for a moment, she saw something flicker in their depths. Doubt? Fear? Or something else entirely?

"I am Li Wei," he said, his voice steady despite the tension in his shoulders. "And I accuse the Demon Empress of crimes against humanity, against the natural order, against—"

"The Witness detects a falsehood," the Arbiter said again.

Li Wei froze. "What? I haven't even made my accusation yet."

"The Witness cannot be fooled by semantics. It detects that you do not believe your own words."

The courtroom went completely silent.

Elara stared at the hero—at Li Wei—with new eyes. *He doesn't believe I'm guilty? Then why—*

"Explain," the head judge demanded.

Li Wei's face was unreadable. "The Witness is mistaken."

"The Witness does not make mistakes."

"I am the hero of this story," Li Wei said, his voice rising. "I have spent years hunting the Demon Empress. I have seen her cruelty firsthand. I have—"

"You have seen what you were told to see."

The voice came from Elara's own mouth, but it wasn't her speaking. It was deeper, older, resonating with a power that made her bones ache.

*What the hell?*

The Witness's eye swiveled to fix on her, and Elara felt something stir in the depths of her soul. Something that had been sleeping. Something that was waking up.

"You are not the Demon Empress," the Witness said, its voice like thunder. "But you are not entirely Elara Chen either."

"What are you talking about?" Elara tried to step back, but her body wouldn't move.

"There is another consciousness within you. One that has been sleeping. One that is now awakening."

The courtroom was in chaos. Judges were rising from their seats. The Arbiter was staring at Elara with something like fear.

And Elara felt it—a presence growing in her chest. Not hostile, but ancient. Powerful. Familiar.

*Oh no.*

"The Demon Empress isn't dead," the Witness continued. "She's been sleeping. And she's waking up."

Elara's vision went white.

When it cleared, she was standing on a battlefield. Corpses littered the ground. The sky was red. And standing before her was a woman who looked exactly like her—but with eyes like molten gold and a smile that promised death.

"Hello, little lawyer," the Demon Empress said. "Did you really think I'd just let you take my body?"
