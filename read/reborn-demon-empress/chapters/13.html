📖 Reborn as the Demon Empress by Luna Nightshade

Chapter 13: The Witness of Truth

~1.8k words · Updated 2026-06-22

The courtroom fell silent. Every spectral face turned toward Elara, waiting for her response. The hero stood across from her, arms crossed, that calculating smile still playing at his lips.

*He knows,* she thought. *Or at least, he suspects.*

The Divine Witness of Truth. A being that could peel back layers of deception like skin from fruit. Under its gaze, she couldn't hide her reincarnation. Couldn't hide that she was Elara Chen, corporate lawyer, not the Demon Empress. Couldn't hide the desperate fear coiling in her gut like a living serpent.

"Objection," Elara said, her voice steady despite the chaos in her chest.

The head judge raised an eyebrow. "On what grounds?"

"Relevance." She turned to face the hero directly. "My identity is not in question. I stand accused of crimes committed by the body I inhabit. Whether I am the original Demon Empress or someone else entirely has no bearing on whether those crimes were committed."

"On the contrary," the hero said smoothly. "If you are not the Demon Empress, then you have no standing to represent her case. The arbitration would be void."

*Clever bastard.*

"Then you admit," Elara countered, "that you're not interested in justice. You're interested in technicalities. In finding a way to invalidate my defense without addressing the actual charges."

The crowd murmured. The hero's smile faltered.

"I'm interested in truth," he said.

"No. You're interested in winning." Elara turned to the judges. "The prosecution has already agreed to this arbitration. They've accepted me as the defendant's representative. To now question my standing is bad faith negotiation. I move to strike the request."

The judges conferred in whispers that sounded like wind through dead leaves.

"The motion is denied," the head judge said finally. "The Divine Witness will be called. However..." He paused, exchanging a glance with his colleagues. "The witness's scope will be limited. It will only compel truth regarding the crimes charged, not the defendant's personal history or origins."

Elara's heart stuttered. *Not good enough. If they start asking about the crimes, they'll ask about my knowledge of them. And I don't have the Demon Empress's memories. I can't answer truthfully without exposing my ignorance.*

"What's wrong?" the hero asked, his voice dripping with false concern. "Afraid the truth will set you free?"

"I'm afraid the truth will be twisted to fit your narrative." Elara's mind raced, searching for an escape, a loophole, anything. "But fine. Call your witness."

*If I can't avoid the trap, I'll have to dismantle it from the inside.*

The Arbiter's voice echoed through the courtroom. "The Divine Witness has been summoned."

The air grew heavy. Pressure built behind Elara's eyes, in her sinuses, in her bones. The cloud walls darkened to storm gray. Lightning flickered at the edges of the starfield ceiling.

And then it appeared.

The Divine Witness was not a being in any recognizable sense. It was a presence, a weight, a *knowing* that filled every corner of the space. Elara felt it slide into her mind like a cold needle, probing, searching, cataloging every thought and memory.

*Easy,* she told herself. *You've done this before. Interrogations. Depositions. You know how to control what you reveal.*

But this was different. This was not a human opponent she could outthink or outmaneuver. This was a force of nature, as indifferent to her strategies as gravity.

The hero stepped forward. "Divine Witness, I ask the defendant: Did you commit the massacre at the Jade Lotus Temple?"

The presence turned its attention to Elara. The pressure intensified. Words rose in her throat, unbidden, fighting to escape.

"I did not," she said, and the words came out true. *Because I wasn't there. Because I wasn't even in this world when it happened.*

The hero's eyes narrowed. "Did you order the massacre?"

Again, the pressure built. Again, the truth struggled to break free.

"I did not." *Because the Demon Empress didn't either. That was a setup.*

"Did you know about the massacre before it happened?"

Elara's jaw clenched. *This is where it gets dangerous.*

"I knew," she said slowly, "that the Demon Empress was being framed for a crime she didn't commit. I didn't know the specifics of the massacre until after I arrived in this body."

The crowd erupted. The hero's face went pale.

"Objection," he snapped. "She's not answering the question."

"She answered," the head judge said, his ancient eyes fixed on Elara with new interest. "She answered completely."

"But—"

"The Witness confirms her truthfulness. Continue."

The hero's composure cracked. For a moment, Elara saw the real man beneath the righteous mask: angry, frustrated, desperate. *He's not used to losing control.*

"Fine." He circled the courtroom, gathering his thoughts. "Let's try another approach. Defendant: Do you currently possess any cultivation ability?"

"No."

"Have you ever cultivated?"

"No."

"Then how do you intend to defend yourself once this arbitration is over? Assuming you win."

Elara saw the trap too late. The question wasn't about her crimes. It was about her future. And the truth was damning.

"I don't know," she admitted. The words felt like ash in her mouth.

"So you're planning to bluff your way through the trial, then what? Run? Hide? Hope your enemies forget about you?"

"I'm planning to use this trial to expose the corruption in the Celestial Sword Sect and the alliances that framed the Demon Empress. I'm planning to build a network of allies and information that will protect me regardless of my cultivation level. I'm planning to survive."

The words tumbled out, raw and unfiltered. The Divine Witness wouldn't let her stop, wouldn't let her soften or qualify.

The hero smiled. "And if those plans fail?"

*Don't answer. Don't—*

"If those plans fail, I'll die. Again."

Silence.

The hero turned to the judges. "I've heard enough. The defendant admits she has no cultivation, no power base, and no real plan for survival. She's admitted she knows she was framed, which means she has knowledge she shouldn't possess. And she's admitted she's not the original Demon Empress."

"I admitted nothing of the sort," Elara said, but the words felt weak.

"You didn't have to. You said 'the Demon Empress was being framed' in third person. And the Witness confirmed you were telling the truth." He spread his hands. "The defendant is an imposter. She has no standing. This entire arbitration is void."

The judges leaned together, whispering. The pressure of the Divine Witness began to recede, but the damage was done.

*I walked right into that,* Elara thought. *He set the entire line of questioning to lead me to that admission.*

But something was wrong. The hero's victory smile was too wide, too triumphant. He was enjoying this too much.

And then she realized.

*He doesn't actually care about the trial. He never did. He just wanted to prove I'm not the Demon Empress.*

"Why?" Elara asked aloud. "Why does it matter who I am?"

The hero's smile faltered. "Excuse me?"

"You've been pushing for this since the beginning. The Divine Witness. The questions about my identity. You don't care about the crimes. You care about me." She stepped closer. "Why?"

For a moment, something flickered in his eyes. Fear. Guilt. And then it was gone, replaced by cold calculation.

"Because the real Demon Empress made a deal with me," he said. "Before she died. Before you took her body."

The words hit Elara like a physical blow.

"A deal?"

"She knew she was going to be executed. She knew she was innocent. And she knew someone would try to take her place." He pulled a scroll from his robes, its surface crackling with dark energy. "She made me promise to find the imposter and deliver this. To the real heir of her throne."

"Her throne?" Elara's blood ran cold. "The Demon Empress had a child?"

"No." The hero's voice dropped to a whisper. "But her sister did. A niece. Hidden away in the mortal realm, raised in secret, trained to take the throne if anything happened to the Empress."

The judges had stopped whispering. Every eye in the courtroom was fixed on the scroll.

"Who killed the Demon Empress?" the head judge asked.

The hero's face twisted. "I did. By her own request."

Chaos erupted. Voices rose in accusation, denial, shock. The Arbiter's voice cut through the noise, but Elara couldn't hear the words. She was staring at the scroll, at the dark energy writhing across its surface.

*She planned this. The Demon Empress planned all of this. Her death. My arrival. The trial.*

*I was never in control.*

The hero held out the scroll. "By the terms of the deal, the imposter's fate is sealed. The throne passes to the rightful heir. And the imposter..." He paused. "The imposter is to be delivered to the heir. Alive."

"Why alive?" Elara forced herself to ask.

"Because the heir wants to meet you." The hero's smile returned, colder than before. "She has questions about the mortal world. About the legal system. About how you managed to outmaneuver entire sects without a drop of cultivation."

*She wants to learn from me.*

*Or she wants to use me.*

"Where is she?" the head judge asked.

The hero turned toward the courtroom doors. They swung open, revealing a figure silhouetted against the storm-light.

"I'm right here," said a voice that was young, feminine, and familiar in a way that made Elara's stomach drop. "Hello, Aunt."

The figure stepped forward, and Elara saw her face.

*Impossible.*

It was her own face. Her mortal face. The face she'd worn for thirty-five years as Elara Chen, corporate lawyer.

The face she'd died with.

"You're wondering how this is possible," the girl said, reading Elara's shock. "The answer is simple. I'm your daughter. Or rather..." She smiled, and it was a predator's smile. "I'm the daughter you never had. The one who was created in a test tube, raised in secret, and sent here to claim what's mine."

"You're not real," Elara whispered.

"Oh, I'm very real. And I have your memories, your skills, your knowledge." She stepped closer, close enough that Elara could see the cold calculation in her eyes. "Everything you learned in the mortal world, I know. Everything you planned to do here, I've already done."

The courtroom dissolved into chaos. The judges were shouting. The Arbiter was trying to restore order. The hero was watching with satisfaction.

And Elara was staring at her own face, her own eyes, her own voice.

"You were never meant to survive this," the girl said softly. "You were meant to be a placeholder. A distraction. A sacrifice."

"For what?" Elara managed.

"For the real Demon Empress's plan. For her revenge. For her return."

The girl's smile widened, and her eyes began to glow with power that Elara recognized.

Cultivation power.

*She's not just the heir,* Elara realized. *She's the weapon.*

"I'm going to destroy the Celestial Sword Sect," the girl said. "I'm going to destroy every sect that betrayed my aunt. And I'm going to use everything you know to do it."

She reached out and touched Elara's forehead.
