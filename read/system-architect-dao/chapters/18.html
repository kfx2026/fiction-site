CHAPTER 18: The Architect's First Blueprint

Kai stared at the System notification, his thumb hovering over the unsent message. Something was wrong. The broadcast had appeared in his vision, but it felt... incomplete. Like a sentence cut off mid-word.

He reached out with his qi sense, trying to probe the notification itself. The moment his spiritual perception touched it, the text flickered, revealing a second layer beneath:

[SYSTEM BROADCAST: TUTORIAL PHASE COMPLETE]
[ALL CLASSES HAVE BEEN ASSIGNED]
[WORLD INTEGRATION: 34.7% COMPLETE]
[ERROR: UNKNOWN CLASS DETECTED - INVESTIGATION PENDING]

Kai's blood ran cold. *Investigation pending.* The System knew about him. Or at least, it knew *something* was wrong.

His phone buzzed again, pulling him back to the present. The group chat had exploded:

*Did everyone see that broadcast? What does "World Integration" mean?*

*I think the System is still installing itself. Like a fucking update.*

*Guys, I'm scared. My tutorial was a forest full of monsters. I almost died three times.*

*At least you got a tutorial. My sister didn't get one at all. She's still Classless.*

Kai scrolled through the messages, his mind racing. Classless. He hadn't considered that possibility. If the System was still integrating, still assigning classes, then maybe—

His phone rang. Unknown number.

He answered, keeping his voice neutral. "Hello?"

"Kai Chen?" The voice was female, professional, with an edge of urgency. "This is Director Zhao from the Beijing Bureau of Anomalous Affairs. We need you to come in for evaluation."

"I'm sorry, what?"

"Your class. It's flagged as anomalous in our systems. We need to verify you're not a threat to national security."

Kai's grip tightened on the phone. "I don't even know what my class does yet. How can it be a threat?"

"Sir, I'm not asking. We have a car en route to your apartment. Please cooperate, and this will go smoothly."

The line went dead.

Kai stared at the phone, his mind blank. The Bureau of Anomalous Affairs. He'd heard rumors on forums and news sites—a government organization formed after the System's arrival, tasked with monitoring and controlling superhuman activity. But he'd never expected to be on their radar.

*Think. What would Mentor do?*

The answer came immediately: *Don't be where they expect you to be.*

Kai grabbed his backpack, stuffing it with essentials—a change of clothes, his phone charger, a half-eaten bag of chips. He paused, then reached out with his qi sense, feeling the subtle flows of energy in his apartment. There. In the corner, near the ceiling, a faint shimmer of qi that hadn't been there before.

A tracking sigil. They'd already marked him.

He had minutes, maybe seconds.

Kai moved. He didn't bother with the door—too obvious. Instead, he focused on the qi in his dantian, still raw and unrefined, and pushed it through his meridians. The sensation was like forcing water through a hose with a kink—painful, inefficient, but possible.

He visualized the wall in front of him, not as a physical barrier, but as a concentration of qi particles held together by the System's architecture. And he saw what Mentor had shown him: the gaps, the weaknesses, the places where the System's hold was thinnest.

*There.*

He slammed his palm against the wall. Qi erupted from his hand, not as a blast, but as a precisely targeted disruption. The plaster cracked, then crumbled, revealing the bricks beneath. Another push, and the bricks shifted, creating a hole just large enough for him to squeeze through.

Kai climbed into the adjacent apartment, hoping it was empty. It was. A studio, sparsely furnished, with dirty dishes in the sink and clothes scattered on the floor. The occupant was clearly in the middle of moving out—or had already abandoned the place.

He didn't stop to investigate. He crossed to the window, opened it, and climbed onto the fire escape. Below, he could see a black sedan pulling up to his building's entrance. Two men in suits got out, their movements efficient, practiced. They didn't look like bureaucrats. They looked like soldiers in civilian clothes.

Kai climbed upward, not down. The roof. From there, he could cross to the next building, then the next, until he was far enough away to think clearly.

His muscles screamed in protest. His qi reserves were nearly empty, drained by the wall-breaking and the climbing. But he kept moving, driven by a single thought: *I can't let them catch me. Not until I understand what I am.*

---

Three hours later, Kai sat in a 24-hour diner on the edge of the city, nursing a cup of coffee he couldn't afford. The place was nearly empty—just a tired waitress and a cook who chain-smoked in the back. The fluorescent lights hummed overhead, casting everything in a sickly yellow glow.

He pulled out his phone, checking for messages. The group chat was still active, but the tone had shifted. Fear had given way to excitement as people shared their new abilities, compared builds, and planned dungeon runs.

*Dungeon runs.* The phrase stuck in his mind. The System had introduced dungeons—instanced spaces where players could fight monsters, earn loot, and level up. It was exactly what every gamer had dreamed of.

But Kai wasn't a gamer anymore. He was something else.

He opened the System interface, navigating to his class page:

[CLASS: Dao Architect]
[STATUS: Active - Anomalous]
[QI CULTIVATION: Meridian Awakening (Stage 1)]
[SKILLS: None]
[BLUEPRINTS: None]

*Blueprints.* That was new. He focused on the word, and a submenu appeared:

[BLUEPRINT: EMPTY]
[CREATE NEW BLUEPRINT?]

Kai's heart raced. This was it. The core of his class. He selected [Yes], and the world around him seemed to fade as a new interface opened:

[BLUEPRINT CREATION]
[MATERIALS REQUIRED: Qi (Variable), Intent (Variable), Understanding (Variable)]
[WARNING: Blueprint creation is permanent. Choose wisely.]

He stared at the prompt, his mind blank. What could he build? What *should* he build?

Mentor's voice echoed in his memory: *"The Dao Architect does not follow the System's rules. You create new rules. You build paths where none exist."*

Kai thought about the Bureau, about the tracking sigil, about the System's investigation flag. He was being hunted, and he had nothing—no skills, no weapons, no allies. If he was going to survive, he needed an edge.

He closed his eyes, reaching deep into his dantian. The qi there was thin, almost depleted, but he could feel its potential. It was raw, formless, waiting to be shaped.

*What do I need?*

The answer came slowly, forming in his mind like a half-remembered dream. A technique. Something simple, something he could use immediately. Something that would let him hide, or escape, or fight.

*Hide. Yes. Hide first.*

He focused on the concept of concealment. Not invisibility—that was too complex, too demanding. Something subtler. A way to make himself unnoticeable, to blend into the background, to be overlooked.

The qi in his dantian began to stir, responding to his intent. It flowed through his meridians, not in a circulation, but in a pattern—a web of energy that connected his body to the world around him. He could feel the diner's qi, the waitress's life force, the hum of the refrigerator in the back.

And he saw how to weave himself into that tapestry.

The blueprint formed in his mind: a lattice of qi threads, each one tuned to a specific frequency. When activated, they would resonate with the ambient energy, creating a field that made his presence seem ordinary, unremarkable. Not invisible. Just... forgettable.

[BLUEPRINT CREATED: Veil of the Unremarkable]
[QI COST: 50 (initial activation) + 5/minute (sustained)]
[DURATION: Until deactivated or qi depleted]
[NOTE: Effectiveness scales with cultivation level. At current stage, deception is limited to mundane observers.]

Kai opened his eyes, gasping. The blueprint burned in his mind, a permanent addition to his consciousness. He could feel it, like a new limb, waiting to be used.

He focused, and the Veil activated. The qi threads spread from his body, weaving into the ambient energy. The waitress glanced at him, then looked away. The cook didn't even turn around. He was just another customer, drinking cheap coffee in a rundown diner.

It worked.

But the cost was staggering. His already depleted qi reserves plummeted, leaving him lightheaded and weak. He'd need hours to recover, and even then, he couldn't sustain the Veil indefinitely.

*It's a start.*

His phone buzzed. Another message, this time from an unknown number:

*You're harder to find than expected. Good. Meet me at the Old North Bridge at midnight. Come alone. - Li Wei*

Kai stared at the message, his mind racing. Li Wei. The name was familiar—a former classmate, a top-tier gamer who'd gone pro years ago. If he was reaching out now, it meant he'd gotten a class too. And if he knew Kai was being hunted...

*This could be a trap.*

But it could also be an opportunity. The Bureau was after him, the System was investigating him, and he had no allies. Li Wei was powerful, connected, and smart. If anyone could help him navigate this new world, it was him.

Kai drained his coffee, left a crumpled bill on the table, and walked out into the night. The Veil clung to him like a second skin, making him forgettable, invisible, safe.

For now.

---

The Old North Bridge was a relic of a bygone era, a stone arch spanning a narrow river that had long since been diverted. It was dark, overgrown, and utterly abandoned—the perfect place for a clandestine meeting.

Kai arrived at 11:57, his qi reserves barely a quarter full. The Veil had drained him more than he'd anticipated, and he'd had to stop twice to rest. But he'd made it.

He waited in the shadows, his senses stretched to their limit. The night was quiet, save for the rustle of leaves and the distant hum of traffic. No one was here.

*Maybe it was a trap after all.*

A footstep. Soft, deliberate. Kai turned, his hands raised, ready to fight or flee.

A figure emerged from the darkness—tall, lean, with sharp eyes that seemed to cut through the night. Li Wei. He looked the same as he had in high school, except for the faint glow emanating from his skin. A cultivator's glow.

"You're early," Li Wei said, his voice calm, measured. "Good. That means you're smart."

"Or desperate," Kai replied. "Why did you call me here?"
