📖 Sword God's Regression by Azure Ink

Chapter 20: The Shadow in the Flame

~2.0k words · Updated 2026-06-22

Jin Tian stood in the empty training courtyard, the afternoon sun casting long shadows across the worn stone. Three hours until his match against Liu Feng. Three hours to prepare for a battle that, in his first life, had ended with a blade through his heart.

But preparation was not what he needed most.

He needed information.

In his first life, Liu Feng had been a respectable opponent—skilled, aggressive, but ultimately predictable. His fire-aspected techniques relied on overwhelming force, not subtlety. He attacked, pressed, and never relented until his opponent broke or he did.

That was the Liu Feng the sect knew.

That was not the man who had murdered him.

Jin Tian closed his eyes, forcing his mind back through the haze of three centuries. The moment of his death was burned into his soul: the cold steel entering his chest, the shock, the betrayal. But beneath the pain, there had been something else. A whisper of black qi, barely visible, coiling around Liu Feng's blade like smoke from a dying flame.

Demon cultivation.

The realization hit him like a physical blow. He had been so focused on his seven disciples, so consumed by their treachery, that he had never questioned how Liu Feng—a mid-ranked inner disciple—had managed to pierce the defenses of a Sword God. Even caught off guard, even betrayed and exhausted, Jin Tian's body had been tempered by centuries of cultivation. No ordinary blade could have killed him.

But demonic qi could.

Liu Feng had been tainted. Or perhaps not tainted—perhaps he had chosen that path willingly. Either way, the man who would face him in three hours was not the same Liu Feng who had fought honorably in the tournaments of Jin Tian's youth.

"Troubling thoughts for one so young."

Jin Tian's eyes snapped open. A figure stood at the edge of the courtyard, leaning against the weathered wall with the casual grace of a predator at rest. He was older than Jin Tian, perhaps eighteen, with sharp features and eyes that held a glint of amusement. His robes marked him as an inner disciple, but Jin Tian did not recognize him.

That was unusual. He knew every inner disciple from his first life, knew their techniques, their strengths, their weaknesses. But this face was unfamiliar.

"I don't believe we've met," Jin Tian said, keeping his voice neutral.

"We haven't." The figure pushed off the wall and walked closer, each step measured and deliberate. "But I've heard quite a bit about you, Jin Tian. The outer disciple who challenged Chen Wei's status. Who dared to claim a slot in the inner division tournament." He stopped a few feet away, tilting his head. "And who now faces Liu Feng."

"You seem well-informed."

"I make it my business to be." The figure smiled, but there was no warmth in it. "My name is Xue Feng. I'm an inner disciple, rank four. And I have a proposition for you."

Jin Tian's blood went cold.

Xue Feng. Rank four inner disciple. A prodigy of the shadow arts, known for his speed and assassination techniques. And in Jin Tian's first life, Xue Feng had been one of his strongest supporters, a loyal ally who had fought beside him in countless battles.

But that Xue Feng had died thirty years before Jin Tian's betrayal, killed in a demon beast outbreak that had claimed half the sect's inner disciples.

If Xue Feng was alive and well, then the timeline had shifted. The butterfly effect of Jin Tian's actions was rippling outward, changing events he had taken for granted.

"What kind of proposition?" he asked, forcing his voice to remain steady.

"The kind that benefits us both." Xue Feng circled him slowly, his movements fluid and silent. "You want to win against Liu Feng. I want to see Liu Feng lose. Our goals align."

"Why?"

Xue Feng's smile faded, replaced by something colder. "Because Liu Feng is not what he appears to be. He has been cultivating something dark, something that the elders would not approve of. I've been watching him for months, gathering evidence. But he's careful. He hides his true strength during matches, never pushing beyond what's acceptable."

Jin Tian's heart raced. Xue Feng knew. Somehow, this version of Xue Feng had discovered the same truth Jin Tian had.

"You think he'll reveal his true power against me."

"I know he will." Xue Feng stopped, facing him directly. "You've humiliated Chen Wei, who is Liu Feng's closest ally. Liu Feng will want to make an example of you. He'll want to hurt you, to break you, to show everyone that crossing his circle has consequences. And to do that, he'll need more than his standard techniques."

The logic was sound. In his first life, Jin Tian had never faced Liu Feng in the inner division tournament—he had been eliminated in the earlier rounds. But now, by changing his path, he had become a target.

"What do you want from me?"

"Survive." Xue Feng's voice dropped to a whisper. "Survive long enough to force him to reveal his demonic cultivation. When he does, I'll be watching. I'll have the evidence I need to bring before the elders. Liu Feng will be expelled, maybe executed. And you'll have your victory."

"And what do you get?"

Xue Feng's eyes glinted. "Justice, Jin Tian. Pure and simple."

The words were too smooth, too rehearsed. In his first life, Xue Feng had been many things—a loyal ally, a deadly fighter, a man of principle. But he had never been simple. There was always a deeper motive, a hidden agenda.

But Jin Tian had no choice. Even if Xue Feng was manipulating him, the immediate threat was Liu Feng. He needed to survive the match first, and then he could worry about the politics.

"Fine," he said. "I'll survive."

"Good." Xue Feng reached into his robe and produced a small jade talisman, carved with intricate characters that Jin Tian recognized as a shielding formation. "Take this. It's a one-use defensive talisman, strong enough to block a single killing strike. Use it when you need it."

Jin Tian took the talisman, feeling the faint hum of qi within. It was genuine, a high-quality artifact that would have cost a small fortune in contribution points.

"Why are you helping me this much?"

"Because I need you alive to expose him." Xue Feng turned and walked away, his voice carrying back over his shoulder. "Don't disappoint me, Jin Tian. And don't trust anyone."

He vanished into the shadows of the corridor, leaving Jin Tian alone with the talisman and a thousand new questions.

---

The arena was packed.

Word had spread quickly through the sect: an outer disciple had challenged the selection process and earned a slot in the inner division tournament. The novelty alone drew a crowd, but the fact that his first opponent was Inner Disciple Liu Feng, rank seven, added a layer of bloodthirsty anticipation.

Jin Tian stood at the edge of the combat platform, his sword at his side, his mind calm. The talisman was tucked into his sleeve, a silent reassurance. He had spent the remaining time reviewing Liu Feng's combat records, analyzing his patterns, memorizing his tells.

But the real battle would not be won with technique.

Across the platform, Liu Feng stepped into the arena. He was taller than Jin Tian remembered, broader in the shoulders, with a mane of dark hair and eyes that burned with barely contained aggression. He wore the standard inner disciple robes, but there was something wrong with the way they hung on his frame—as if they were hiding something beneath.

"So," Liu Feng said, his voice carrying easily across the arena, "the little rat who thinks he can play with the big dogs." He drew his blade, a curved saber that gleamed with a faint red tinge. "I'm going to enjoy breaking you."

Jin Tian didn't respond. He drew his own sword, a simple outer disciple blade, and settled into his stance.

The overseer raised his hand. "The match will end when one combatant yields, is incapacitated, or is thrown from the platform. Lethal force is prohibited." His eyes swept across both fighters. "Begin."

Liu Feng moved first.

He exploded forward, his saber trailing a wake of crimson qi that left scorch marks on the stone. The crowd gasped at the speed, at the raw power. Jin Tian's eyes narrowed.

Faster than the records. Stronger.

He sidestepped, letting the saber pass inches from his chest. The heat from the blade washed over him, uncomfortably warm. Liu Feng didn't pause, spinning into a second strike that forced Jin Tian to parry.

The impact jarred up his arm. Liu Feng's strength was abnormally high, even for an inner disciple. The demonic cultivation had already begun to transform his body.

Jin Tian retreated, buying space. Liu Feng pressed, relentless, his saber becoming a storm of crimson light. Each strike was precise, each combination designed to corner and crush.

But Jin Tian had faced better.

He flowed around the attacks, deflecting where necessary, dodging where possible. His movements were minimal, efficient, conserving energy while Liu Feng burned through his.

"You can't run forever!" Liu Feng snarled, his attacks growing more desperate.

"I'm not running." Jin Tian's voice was calm. "I'm waiting."

"Waiting for what?"

"For you to show me what you're hiding."

Liu Feng's eyes widened, a flicker of panic crossing his face. He recovered quickly, masking it with aggression, but Jin Tian had seen it.

He knew.

The match continued, a brutal dance of offense and defense. Liu Feng's attacks grew wilder, less controlled. The crowd watched in stunned silence, unable to believe that an outer disciple was holding his own against a ranked inner disciple.

And then it happened.

Liu Feng's saber paused mid-swing. His body went rigid, and a wave of black qi erupted from his pores, swirling around him like a living shadow. The temperature in the arena dropped, and the spectators gasped as the black energy spread across the platform, staining the stone with its corruption.

"You wanted to see," Liu Feng whispered, his voice distorted, layered with something inhuman. "Now you will die."

He moved.

The speed was blinding, beyond what any inner disciple should have been capable of. Jin Tian barely had time to raise his sword before the black-tinged saber descended.

The talisman in his sleeve flared to life, creating a barrier of golden light that absorbed the impact. The shockwave shattered nearby stones and sent the audience stumbling backward.

Liu Feng's eyes widened in surprise.

And in that moment of distraction, Jin Tian struck.

His sword moved in a perfect arc, the same technique he had used to kill a Demon Lord in his first life. The blade found the gap in Liu Feng's guard, sliding past the black qi, and pierced his shoulder.

Liu Feng screamed, a sound that was more beast than human. The black qi recoiled, retreating into his body as he stumbled backward, clutching his wound.

The arena was silent.

Jin Tian stood over him, his sword dripping blood, his eyes cold. "Yield."

Liu Feng looked up at him, and for a moment, Jin Tian saw the man he had once known—the honorable fighter, the worthy rival. But it was only a flicker, quickly extinguished by the darkness within.

"I yield," Liu Feng whispered, his voice broken.

The crowd erupted.

Jin Tian turned away, his mind already racing. He had won. Liu Feng's demonic cultivation had been revealed. But instead of relief, he felt a growing unease.

Xue Feng had promised to be watching. He had promised to bring Liu Feng to justice.
